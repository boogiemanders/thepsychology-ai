# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Psychology doctoral students and postdocs preparing for the EPPP licensure exam. They are clinically trained, analytically minded, and skeptical of marketing. They're under pressure — financially stressed, anxious about passing, and time-constrained. They use this product in focused study sessions where clarity and speed matter. The job to be done: pass the EPPP efficiently with confidence, not hope.

### Brand Personality
**Direct. Precise. Confident.**

Voice sounds like a psychologist who's been through the worst of EPPP prep talking to someone in the middle of it. Leads with vulnerability and data, never with hype. Clinical precision meets real-talk honesty. Never sells — shares what actually happened, with numbers to back it up.

Emotional target: **calm confidence** — "I'm in good hands. This tool respects my intelligence and my time."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Dark, sharp, fast-feeling. Premium technical authority. The interface should feel like a precision instrument, not a consumer app.

**References:**
- [linear.app](https://linear.app) — minimal, confident, developer-grade speed and polish. Closest match for overall feel.
- [micro1.ai](https://www.micro1.ai/) — modern dark aesthetic, sharp typography, authoritative presence.
- Duolingo — liked for gamification mechanics, but too colorful. Borrow the engagement patterns, not the palette.

**Anti-references:**
- Headspace/Calm/Oura — too soft, too passive. This audience needs authority, not serenity.
- SimplePractice/TherapyNotes — clinical but dated. Not the standard.
- Generic SaaS dashboards — bordered card grids, scattered colors, feature-dump layouts.
- Anything that looks like "startup marketing" — gradients, floating mockups, empty promises.

**Theme:** Dark mode primary. Light mode supported. The brand palette (sage, olive, coral, dusty rose, lavender gray, soft blue) is used sparingly for semantic meaning (priority levels, status indicators), not decoration.

**Special context:** The quiz/test mode uses an intentional Windows 2000 retro theme to mirror the look and feel of the real EPPP testing environment. This is deliberate and should be preserved.

### Typography
- **UI text:** Geist Sans (Helvetica Neue fallback) — clean, modern, no-nonsense
- **Code/data:** Geist Mono — metrics, scores, IDs, timestamps
- **Exam questions:** Merriweather (serif) — professional, readable at length
- **Quiz mode:** Tahoma — matches real EPPP testing software aesthetic
- **Spacing:** Generous line-height (1.8) in content areas. Tight in UI chrome.

### Color System
Brand colors have semantic meaning — don't use them decoratively:
- **Sage (#bdd1ca):** Low priority, diagnostic, review. Also used for text selection highlight.
- **Olive (#788c5d):** Medium priority, practice sections.
- **Coral (#d87758):** High priority, study mode, highlight accent (light mode).
- **Dusty Rose (#c46685):** Critical priority, test mode.
- **Lavender Gray (#cbc9db):** Secondary accent.
- **Soft Blue (#6a9bcc):** Tertiary accent.

UI chrome uses neutral oklch tokens (zinc/neutral scale). One accent color at a time. Clear borders over background tints.

### Design Principles

1. **Respect the user's intelligence.** No hand-holding UI, no patronizing tooltips, no "Are you sure?" for routine actions. These are doctoral-level clinicians. Design for competence.

2. **Speed is a feature.** Every interaction should feel instant. Prefer skeleton states over spinners. Reduce clicks. The interface should never be the bottleneck — the exam content is hard enough.

3. **Data over decoration.** Show scores, progress, and domain breakdowns with clinical precision. Numbers and charts earn trust. Gradients and illustrations don't. If it doesn't inform a study decision, it doesn't belong.

4. **Semantic color, not decorative color.** Brand colors signal meaning (priority, status, section identity). The rest of the UI is neutral. One accent at a time. Never rainbow.

5. **Earn authority through restraint.** White space, sharp typography, and consistent density signal competence. Cluttered screens signal amateur hour. When in doubt, remove.

### Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline (no specific requirements stated, but the audience includes people in extended study sessions — eye strain matters)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (already implemented for marquee)
- Dark mode is primary — ensure sufficient contrast on dark backgrounds
- Exam content must be highly readable at sustained reading lengths

### Component Patterns
- **Framework:** shadcn/ui (new-york style) + Radix primitives
- **Animations:** Framer Motion / Motion — purposeful, fast, never gratuitous
- **Icons:** Lucide React
- **Charts:** Recharts
- **Layout:** Let type, spacing, and composition create hierarchy. Cards for content grouping. Tabs for mode switching. Minimal nesting.
