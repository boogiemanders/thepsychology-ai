# Positioning Angles: Digital Executive Functioning Assessment Platform

## Context

**What we're selling:** A digital executive functioning assessment platform that replaces broken, outdated tools (CPT-3, TOVA, Pearson Q-interactive) with modern, accurate, remote-capable assessments — integrated with insurance verification and instant booking.

**The transformation:**
- Clinician: From 10-20 hours per evaluation (scoring by hand, writing reports, fighting insurance) → streamlined digital workflow with AI-assisted reports, transparent scoring, and integrated billing
- Patient: From 5-10 month wait, $2,000-$7,000 cost, 4-8 hours of boring clicking → accessible, affordable, remote assessment that actually predicts real-world functioning
- The field: From 1993 norms based on 250 Caucasians → diverse, continuously updated normative data

**Alternatives (what people do today):**
1. **CPT-3/TOVA** — coin-flip accuracy (51.7%), proprietary hardware, VGA cables in 2026, breaks with Windows updates
2. **Pearson Q-interactive** — crashes mid-test, "black box" scoring, subscription creep, 1-star App Store reviews
3. **Paper-and-pencil batteries** — 5-10 hours scoring/reporting, examiner drift, no remote option
4. **Do nothing** — 5-10 month wait lists, patients give up or go undiagnosed
5. **Digital disruptors (Creyos, BrainCheck)** — rushed to market, weak validation, no neuropsychologists on team

**Unique mechanism:** [To be named — candidates below]
- Psychology-trained AI that understands the constructs being measured (built by psychologists who passed the EPPP, not just engineers)
- Ecologically valid digital tasks that measure real-world executive function, not just button-pressing in a sterile lab
- Continuous normative engine — norms update as the sample grows, unlike frozen 1993 datasets
- EPPP-to-provider pipeline — the clinicians who use our assessment tools studied on our platform

**Market sophistication:** Stage 3 (Crowded/Skeptical). Clinicians have seen digital assessment promises before. BrainCheck disappointed. Q-interactive broke trust. They need to understand WHY this is different. Mechanism and proof are essential.

## Angle Options

### Angle 1: The Broken Science Angle (Enemy)

- **The angle:** The tools clinicians rely on for ADHD and executive function diagnosis are statistically no better than a coin flip — and the field knows it but hasn't had an alternative.
- **Why it works:** This isn't provocative opinion — it's published data. CPT-3 correctly classifies only 51.7% of ADHD patients. TOVA's norms are from 250 Caucasians in 1993. Board-certified neuropsychologists on Student Doctor Network call these tools "god-awful terrible." Clinicians already suspect this. Saying it out loud builds instant credibility.
- **Headline direction:**
  - "Your attention test is a coin flip. Here's one that isn't."
  - "51.7% accuracy. That's what your CPT-3 actually delivers."
  - "The TOVA's normative data is from 1993. Your patients deserve better."
- **When to use:** Conference talks, academic marketing, clinician-facing content. This is the angle that gets shared in neuropsych forums and cited in journal articles. Strongest with early-adopter clinicians who already distrust current tools.

---

### Angle 2: The Modern Software Angle (Contrarian)

- **The angle:** In 2026, neuropsychological assessment still requires VGA cables, proprietary hardware that reboots your computer, and scoring software that crashes mid-test. We built what Pearson and TOVA should have built 15 years ago.
- **Why it works:** The absurdity is the hook. Every clinician has a Q-interactive horror story. Every TOVA user has lost a test session to a Windows update. Clinicians don't need to be convinced the problem exists — they live it. This angle turns shared frustration into switching energy.
- **Headline direction:**
  - "No VGA cables. No proprietary hardware. No software that crashes during your patient's test."
  - "Q-interactive has 1-star reviews. We thought clinicians deserved better."
  - "Executive function assessment that works on hardware from this decade."
- **When to use:** Product marketing, landing pages, social media. This is the angle that makes clinicians laugh, nod, and share. Best for driving awareness and trial. Pair with a free demo that shows the contrast immediately.

---

### Angle 3: The Clinician Time Angle (Speed/Ease)

- **The angle:** A neuropsych evaluation takes 10-20 hours of professional time — most of it scoring, reporting, and fighting insurance. We cut that to 2 hours.
- **Why it works:** Clinician time is the bottleneck. 73% of referral sources say slow turnaround hurts patient care. Clinicians spend 5-10 hours writing reports referral sources don't even read. The math is brutal: at $200/hour opportunity cost, every evaluation burns $2,000-$4,000 in clinician time alone. This angle appeals to the business-owner side of every private practice clinician.
- **Headline direction:**
  - "10 hours to score and write one report. Or 45 minutes."
  - "Your time is worth $200/hour. Stop spending it on hand scoring."
  - "See 3x more patients. Same hours. Better reports."
- **When to use:** Practice-building content, provider subscription marketing, conference presentations on practice efficiency. Strongest with private practice clinicians who feel the time squeeze.

---

### Angle 4: The Access & Equity Angle (Transformation)

- **The angle:** 5-10 month wait for adults. 1+ year for children. $2,000-$7,000 out of pocket. And the norms are based on 250 white college students from Minnesota in 1993. Executive function assessment needs to work for everyone, not just people who can afford to wait and pay.
- **Why it works:** This hits on two levels — the practical barrier (cost/wait) and the moral one (cultural bias in norms). It positions the product as a mission, not just a tool. For clinicians who went into psychology to help people, this is the angle that resonates with their identity. For patients, it's "finally, someone gets it."
- **Headline direction:**
  - "Executive function assessment that doesn't require a 10-month wait and a $5,000 bill."
  - "Built for every brain. Not just the 250 Caucasians from 1993."
  - "Your patients can't wait 10 months. Now they don't have to."
- **When to use:** Mission-driven marketing, grant applications, institutional partnerships, DEI-focused clinical programs. Strongest with community mental health, school psychologists, and clinicians serving underserved populations.

---

### Angle 5: The Pipeline Angle (Specificity / Social Proof)

- **The angle:** Built by psychologists who passed the EPPP on this platform, validated by the clinicians who use it, and continuously improved by the outcomes it measures. This isn't a tech company's guess at what clinicians need — it's what the profession built for itself.
- **Why it works:** BrainCheck was criticized for having no neuropsychologists on their team. Pearson is a publishing corporation. TOVA is a family company selling hardware from the 90s. The credibility gap is real. This angle says: "We're one of you." The EPPP-to-provider pipeline is a genuine differentiator no competitor can replicate.
- **Headline direction:**
  - "Built by the psychologists who studied on this platform. Used by the clinicians who graduated from it."
  - "From EPPP prep to clinical practice — assessment tools built by the profession, for the profession."
  - "Not a tech company guessing at clinical needs. A psychology platform that grew up."
- **When to use:** Professional credibility contexts — APA presentations, journal ads, professional association partnerships. Strongest with skeptical senior clinicians and academic programs. Also strong for provider recruitment (the "join us" message).

---

## Recommended Starting Points

**For clinician acquisition (provider-side):** Start with **Angle 2 (Modern Software)** — it's the lowest-friction entry point. Every clinician has a war story. Pair it immediately with **Angle 3 (Time)** to give them a reason to switch, not just commiserate.

**For patient acquisition (client-side):** Lead with **Angle 4 (Access & Equity)** — cost, wait time, and the promise of assessment that actually represents them.

**For professional credibility / conference circuit:** Lead with **Angle 1 (Broken Science)** backed by **Angle 5 (Pipeline)** — "the science says current tools don't work, and here's why we're positioned to build what does."

**For fundraising / grants:** **Angle 4 (Access)** + **Angle 1 (Broken Science)** + validation roadmap from OpenEvidence research.

## Naming the Mechanism

The mechanism needs a name. Options:

1. **Adaptive Ecological Assessment (AEA)** — emphasizes ecological validity + adaptive testing
2. **Living Norms Engine** — emphasizes continuously updated normative data vs. frozen 1993 datasets
3. **Multi-Construct Profiling** — emphasizes measuring multiple EF constructs vs. single attention metric
4. **The [Platform Name] Method** — simple eponymous naming

Recommendation: Name the normative data approach ("Living Norms") and the assessment methodology separately. "Living Norms" is the most immediately differentiated — nothing else updates norms in real-time. The assessment methodology name should come after the specific tasks are designed.
