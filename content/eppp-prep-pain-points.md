# EPPP Prep Program Pain Points — thepsychology.ai Pitch Ammunition

## Context
Research into real user complaints (Reddit, Student Doctor Network, Trustpilot, BBB, articles) about EPPP prep competitors to identify specific pain points thepsychology.ai can exploit in its pitch. Data sourced from forum threads, review sites, and industry articles.

# CROSS-CUTTING THEMES (Pitch Ammunition)

## 1. The Prep Industry Is Overpriced
AATBS charges $849-$1,799 for materials. Add retakes ($687.50 each) and multiple programs, and candidates can spend $5,000-$8,000+ before passing. Early-career psychologists are already in financial hardship.

## 2. Content Is Outdated and Dense
AATBS materials are "cumbersome, intimidating, and overly detailed." One person passed with 11-year-old PsychPrep materials — suggesting the content barely changes. Materials contain contradictions and outdated information.

## 3. Practice Tests Don't Match the Real Exam
Every program gets this complaint. Practice scores don't predict real scores. Question phrasing differs. Content appears on the real exam that isn't in any study guide. Students scoring 80-90% on practice tests still fail.

## 4. No One Addresses Test-Taking Strategy
The #1 predictor of EPPP success is practice testing, not content review. Yet most programs sell content review as their primary product. Only PsychPrep is consistently praised for teaching test-taking strategies.

## 5. The Emotional Toll Is Devastating
People describe "deep depression," feeling "defeated, beyond defeated," financial hardship, and anxiety. The system creates a mental health crisis for people trying to become mental health providers. The irony is brutal.

## 6. Racial Bias Is Structural
Black test-takers fail at 2.7x the rate of white test-takers. The exam differentiates more by race than by clinical competence. No prep company addresses this disparity.

## 7. Generic Companies Produce Garbage
ExamEdge and Mometrix are generic test prep companies with no psychology expertise. Their materials contain factual errors, PSY 101-level questions, and create false confidence.

## 8. The SEPPO Is the Best-Kept Secret
Retired EPPP questions (SEPPO) cost only $30 per test and are the closest to the actual exam — but most students don't know about them. The prep industry doesn't promote this because it would undercut their products.
