## Draft

The Therabot study ran a real randomized trial on a GenAI therapy chatbot. Here is what it found.

Randomized controlled trial. 210 adults. Four weeks. Published in NEJM AI in 2025.

Therabot produced large, statistically significant reductions in depression (effect size d = 0.85-0.90) and anxiety (d = 0.79-0.84) compared to a waitlist control, sustained at 8-week follow-up (Stade et al., 2025).

Participants rated their therapeutic alliance with the chatbot comparably to outpatient therapy clients.

Here is what this does not mean.

The control was a waitlist, not a therapist. Therabot outperformed doing nothing. That is meaningful. It is also a much smaller claim than AI replaces therapy.

The trial excluded people with active suicidality. The population was mild-to-moderate presentations.

What it does mean: AI tools are showing real clinical signal for mild-to-moderate symptoms as a supplement or bridge to care. The evidence does not support autonomous treatment of moderate-to-severe conditions.

That distinction matters when clients start asking you about these tools.
