Most candidates divide their study time equally across all 8 domains. That feels thorough. It is one of the most expensive mistakes you can make.

VALUE (8-50s):
Here is the formula.

Take each EPPP domain. Score your weakness from 1 to 5. One is strong, five is weak.

Multiply that weakness score by the domain's exam weight.

The domains with the highest score get 70 percent of your study time.

The domains you are already strong in get maintenance only. One practice block a week.

For most candidates, the domains that rank highest on weakness times weight are Biological Bases and Clinical Psychology because they combine high exam weight with common knowledge gaps. But your diagnostic tells you YOUR specific ranking. That number is what drives your schedule, not a generic priority list.

This is weighted priority studying. The adaptive platform at thePsychology.ai runs this calculation automatically after your diagnostic. You can also do it manually right now with any score report.

PAYOFF:
When you stop dividing time equally and start dividing it by weakness times weight, every hour you study actually moves your score.
