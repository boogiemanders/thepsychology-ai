# When you can sit the EPPP is being renegotiated right now.

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

When you can sit the EPPP is being renegotiated right now.

Today in California, you can test after finishing your doctoral coursework. You do not need to complete all post-doctoral hours first.

Two forces are pushing in opposite directions.

ASPPB originally wanted to require full post-doctoral completion before testing, tied to the EPPP2 proposal. That proposal was abandoned in October 2024 (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

Going the other direction: California's AB 282, sponsored by the CPA, would formally put eligibility decisions with states rather than ASPPB and codify the after-coursework standard (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

Why it matters: the California Board believes earlier testing could improve pass rates. The 2025 first-time pass rate was 58.7%. Overall: 36.5%.

If you are in active prep, nothing changes today. But if you are a trainee mapping a two-year timeline, this is worth watching.

The one constant: what the exam tests. Clinical reasoning across the domains. That does not change based on eligibility policy.

Focus your prep there.
