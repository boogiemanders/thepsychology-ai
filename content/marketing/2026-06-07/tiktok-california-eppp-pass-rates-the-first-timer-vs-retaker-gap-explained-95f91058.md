"The overall rate is dragged down by retakers. First-time candidates pass at 58.7%. Retakers pass at significantly lower rates.

Why? Because most retakers go back and study the exact same way they studied the first time.

Same inputs. Same methods. Different expectations.

Here is what the data tells you.

First: take a fresh diagnostic before you study anything. Not review material. A cold diagnostic shows where your gaps actually are right now, not where you think they are.

Second: your weakest two or three domains need 70% of your time. Not equal time across eight domains. Your weakest domains, every week.

Third: switch from reading to active quizzing. The EPPP tests application under pressure, not what you read in a textbook. Practice questions are the whole game.

The candidates in that 58.7% group are not smarter. They just do these three things consistently."
