"The pandemic was a collective disruption. It divided how most people experience time, into before and after.

The 'before' period gets encoded as safe, even if it was not perfect. 2016 had its own problems. But the disruption had not hit yet, so it feels lighter in retrospect.

Research on nostalgia is consistent: nostalgia increases during periods of social instability. It functions as an emotional stabilizer. It helps people maintain a sense of continuity when the present keeps shifting.

When someone says 2016 was better, they are not really talking about 2016. They are saying: I am exhausted. I want to feel intact again.

That is a legitimate feeling. Most people dismiss it as sentimentality. It is actually a coping response, your brain looking for an anchor when the present feels unstable."
