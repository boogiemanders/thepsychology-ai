## Draft

Your patients are probably already using AI chatbots for mental health support (WBUR, 2026).

Here is what the research actually shows.

Published RCT evidence shows some AI chatbots produce real symptom reductions in major depression and generalized anxiety disorder (NEJM AI, 2024). These are not pilot studies. These are randomized controlled trials.

At the same time:

AI chatbots cannot reliably detect suicidal ideation or psychiatric emergencies. Research consistently shows human therapists outperform chatbots for moderate to severe presentations (Simply Psychology, 2026). Most studies run 2 to 8 weeks. Long-term outcomes are not yet established.

These tools are not replacing therapy. They are filling the gap between sessions, on waitlists, and at 2 a.m. when a human is not available.

The question for clinicians is not whether patients are using them. It is whether you are prepared to counsel them on when it helps and when it does not.
