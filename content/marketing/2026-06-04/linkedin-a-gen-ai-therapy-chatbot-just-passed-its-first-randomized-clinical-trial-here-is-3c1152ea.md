# A Gen-AI therapy chatbot just passed its first randomized clinical trial. Here is what the data actually says.

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

A randomized controlled trial of a generative AI therapy chatbot just published in NEJM AI.

First of its kind. Real clinical-level symptoms: depression, anxiety, and eating disorders.

Here is what the study actually shows, beyond the headline.

Measurable symptom reductions. That is meaningful. It is also not the full picture.

Across multiple studies, AI chatbots consistently underperform human therapists for moderate-to-severe presentations. And current AI chatbots cannot reliably detect suicidal ideation or psychiatric emergencies. That is a significant gap.

So what is this technology actually suited for?

Reaching people who would never seek care otherwise. Not as a replacement for a therapist. As an accessible first point of contact.

Two frames the evidence does not support:

"AI will replace therapists." No.
"AI has no role in mental health care." Also no.

The frame that actually fits the data: AI handles access, therapists handle complexity.

What is your read? How are you thinking about AI tools in your clinical practice?
