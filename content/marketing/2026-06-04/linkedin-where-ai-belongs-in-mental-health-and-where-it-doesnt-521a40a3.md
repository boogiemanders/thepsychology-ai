# Where AI belongs in mental health (and where it doesn't)

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

Most AI mental health apps cannot reliably detect a mental health crisis.

A 2026 review of AI therapy chatbot research found that current chatbots are unable to consistently identify suicidal ideation or psychiatric emergencies. They work well for psychoeducation, teaching users CBT principles and emotional regulation skills. For moderate to severe conditions, human therapists produce significantly better outcomes.

That gap matters. And it is worth saying plainly, especially if you are a clinician evaluating these tools for your practice.

AI in mental health is not one thing. There is a real difference between a tool that teaches grounding techniques and one that is supposed to assess clinical risk. Conflating the two is where harm happens.

At thepsychology.ai, we build for a contained use case: EPPP exam prep. Not open-ended therapy. Not crisis support. The scope is clear, the evidence basis is clear, and we are not trying to replace clinical judgment.

More builders in this space should say that out loud.
