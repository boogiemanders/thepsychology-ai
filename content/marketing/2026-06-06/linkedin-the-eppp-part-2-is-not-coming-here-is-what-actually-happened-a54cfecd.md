# The EPPP Part 2 is not coming. Here is what actually happened.

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

The EPPP Part 2 is not coming. Here is what actually happened, and what it means if you are studying right now.

ASPPB formally abandoned the EPPP2 after pausing it in October 2024. The California Board of Psychology reviewed the full history at its February 2026 board meeting.

Here is what the record shows:
- May 2023: the board approved rules for an EPPP Part 2 Skills Exam, set for January 2026.
- October 2024: ASPPB paused the plan.
- ASPPB then abandoned the EPPP2 entirely, forcing the board to revisit everything it had approved.
- 2027 is the earliest possible window if ASPPB revives any second-exam plan. Nothing is confirmed.

What this means for you: the EPPP you are preparing for is unchanged. Same format, same eight content domains, same scoring.

The one exception: four jurisdictions still require Part 2 on top of Part 1, the District of Columbia, Georgia, Guam, and Nevada. Everywhere else, Part 1 is the whole exam.

One open question remains: eligibility. A proposal would require future candidates to finish all post-doctoral hours before testing. That rule has not changed yet.

California's 2025 first-time pass rate was 58.7%. If you are in prep right now, the exam in front of you is the one you have.
