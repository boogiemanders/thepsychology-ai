# The EPPP is changing. Here is what the data actually says.

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

> [!warning] Needs human review
> The CA Board facts file (p. 120, 29) confirms EPPP2 abandoned and 2027 as earliest window. The framing of the replacement as a 'single integrated exam combining knowledge and skills' comes from a web search summary of the ASPPB page, not a direct read. Verify asppb.net confirms: (1) EPPP2 fully abandoned not just paused, (2) new single-exam format confirmed, (3) 2027 window still current before publishing.

## Draft

The EPPP2, the two-part skills exam ASPPB had planned for January 2026, has been abandoned.

That is not a rumor. It is in the California Board of Psychology meeting materials from February 13, 2026.

What replaces it is still being built. ASPPB is developing a new exam that combines knowledge and skills into a single test. The earliest possible window for that launch is 2027. Nothing is confirmed beyond that.

Here is what this means if you are studying right now:

The exam you are preparing for has not changed. Study for the current EPPP.

California's 2025 numbers are worth knowing: 58.7% first-time pass rate, 36.5% overall. Both are trending lower than the four years before them.

If you are sitting the exam in 2026, nothing shifts for you. If you are years out, watch the ASPPB official announcements, not social media.

The one thing that does not change: targeted, diagnostic prep outperforms passive studying regardless of what the exam format looks like.
