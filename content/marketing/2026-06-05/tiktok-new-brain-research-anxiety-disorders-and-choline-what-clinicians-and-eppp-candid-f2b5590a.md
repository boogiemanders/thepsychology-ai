## VALUE (8-50s)

A major brain scan analysis published in May 2026 found that people with anxiety disorders have significantly lower levels of choline compared to non-anxious controls.

Choline is essential for healthy brain cell membrane function and for producing acetylcholine, the neurotransmitter involved in attention, memory, and stress regulation.

The research suggests that differences in choline levels may be part of what distinguishes anxious brains at a structural and functional level.

For EPPP candidates: the biological bases of behavior domain tests neurotransmitter mechanisms. The role of acetylcholine in cognition and stress regulation is fair game on the exam. This is a concrete current example.

For clinicians: this is early-stage evidence, not a treatment protocol. But it adds to the picture of anxiety as partly neurobiological, and it matters for how we explain presentations to clients.
