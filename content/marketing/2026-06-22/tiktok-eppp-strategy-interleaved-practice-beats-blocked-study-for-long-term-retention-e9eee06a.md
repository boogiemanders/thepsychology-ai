# EPPP Strategy: Interleaved practice beats blocked study for long-term retention

**TikTok script · EPPP Strategy**

## Draft

Here's the strategy I used to cover all twelve E triple P domains without burning out on any one of them. I call it topic switching.

Most candidates block their study. One full week on biopsychology, then one full week on assessment. Feels efficient. But a 2025 study in Applied Cognitive Psychology found that switching between topics within a single session, called interleaved practice, produces better long-term retention than blocking one topic at a time.

The reason is that when you switch, your brain cannot rely on context or short-term memory to retrieve the answer. It has to build a real retrieval route. The exam will mix all twelve domains on you. Your study schedule should do the same thing.

Rotate three to four domains per session, fifteen to twenty minutes each. The session feels harder. Your score on test day feels easier.
