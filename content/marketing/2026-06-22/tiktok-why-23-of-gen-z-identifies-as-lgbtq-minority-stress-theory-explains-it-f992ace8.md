## Draft

A psychologist explains why 23 percent of Gen Z now identifies as LGBTQ plus, and what that statistic actually tells us.

Gallup data shows Gen Z identifies as LGBTQ plus at three times the rate of Millennials. The backlash take says this is social contagion. The data says something else.

The minority stress model, developed by Ilan Meyer in 2003, explains that people with stigmatized identities carry two layers of stress: the same stressors everyone faces, plus a second layer of minority-specific ones. Stigma. Concealment. Discrimination. Each one is a chronic cognitive load.

What has changed in Gen Z is not the underlying rate of LGBTQ plus identity. It is the willingness to name it. When language becomes available and disclosure feels safer, more people disclose. The rate of disclosure goes up. The rate of identity does not suddenly change.

That is not contagion. That is what minority stress theory has predicted for twenty years.
