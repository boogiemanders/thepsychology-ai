# EPPP Practice Question: Confirmation bias in social cognition

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

If you want to instantly answer any E triple P question about confirmation bias, this is the only video you will ever need.

Which of the following best shows confirmation bias? A: remembering events that contradict your beliefs but discounting ones that support them. B: overestimating the relationship between two unrelated variables. C: believing you can influence a random event by your actions. D: seeking out news sources that align with your political views and ignoring opposing ones.


The answer is D. Confirmation bias is seeking information that confirms what you already believe, and filtering out what challenges it. A is wrong because it reverses the direction. B is wrong because that is illusory correlation, seeing a pattern that is not there. C is wrong because that is illusion of control, a different bias entirely.
