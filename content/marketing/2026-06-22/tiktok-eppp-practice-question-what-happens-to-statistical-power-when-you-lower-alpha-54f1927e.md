# EPPP Practice Question: What happens to statistical power when you lower alpha?

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here's why you should always know what happens to power when you change alpha on E triple P.

A researcher changes the significance level from point zero five to point zero one, keeping sample size, effect size, and test the same. What is the most likely result? A: power will increase and Type I error will decrease. B: effect size will increase because the criterion is more conservative. C: power will decrease and false positives will become less likely. D: the sampling distribution will become more normal.


The answer is C. Stricter alpha makes the null harder to reject. That cuts your power. The upside is fewer false positives. A is wrong because power goes in the opposite direction when you tighten alpha. B is wrong because effect size is a property of the real world, not a dial you set with alpha. D is wrong because alpha does not touch the shape of the sampling distribution at all.
