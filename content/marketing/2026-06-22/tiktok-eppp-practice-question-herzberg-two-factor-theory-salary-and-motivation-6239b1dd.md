# EPPP Practice Question: Herzberg two-factor theory, salary and motivation

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Today we're talking about how to get Herzberg questions right on E triple P.

A company raises salaries and improves the break room. Six months later, enthusiasm for the work itself has not changed. Which factor did they improve? A: motivator factors, which directly increase satisfaction. B: valence, which guarantees higher motivation if rewards are visible. C: hygiene factors, which reduce dissatisfaction but do not create motivation. D: task identity, which automatically produces job enrichment.


The answer is C. Herzberg said salary and working conditions are hygiene factors. Adequate hygiene factors stop the bleeding. They do not generate motivation. A is wrong because motivators are responsibility, growth, and the work itself. B is wrong because valence belongs to Vroom's expectancy theory, a completely different model. D is wrong because task identity comes from Hackman and Oldham's job characteristics model, not Herzberg.
