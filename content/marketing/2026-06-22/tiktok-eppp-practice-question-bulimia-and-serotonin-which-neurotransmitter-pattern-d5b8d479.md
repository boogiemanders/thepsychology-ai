# EPPP Practice Question: Bulimia and serotonin, which neurotransmitter pattern?

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here's the single easiest way to stop confusing anorexia and bulimia serotonin patterns on E triple P.

A 19-year-old presents with binge-purge episodes consistent with bulimia nervosa. Based on the neurobiology, which pattern is most likely? A: elevated dopamine in the mesolimbic pathway. B: deficient serotonin in specific brain regions. C: elevated serotonin causing anxiety. D: deficient GABA producing chronic insomnia.


The answer is B. Low serotonin drives bulimia. The bingeing is the brain trying to spike serotonin the fast way. A is wrong because mesolimbic dopamine is the reward and addiction circuit, not the eating-disorder one. C is wrong because elevated serotonin describes anorexia. Restricting food temporarily lowers serotonin, which is paradoxically calming for that population. D is wrong because GABA is the brake pedal, not the binge driver.
