## Draft

Here's why AI actually helps musicians create more, not less, and why it only works through one specific psychological mechanism.

Producers and composers worry AI will replace them. A 2025 study found that AI did enhance musical creativity, but only when it boosted one thing: self-efficacy.

Bandura defined self-efficacy as belief in your ability to complete a task. When musicians believed they could work the AI tool effectively, their creative output went up. When they doubted it, output dropped. The AI was not the creative agent. The musician's confidence in using it was.

This maps directly onto what we know about creative blocks. The block is almost never a lack of talent. It is a collapse of confidence in the process. Every producer who has sat at a blank session knows that feeling.

The tool changes. The psychology does not.
