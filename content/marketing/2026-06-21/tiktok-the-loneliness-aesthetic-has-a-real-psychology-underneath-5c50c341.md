## Draft

If your entire aesthetic right now is I prefer to be alone, this is worth knowing.

The loneliness aesthetic is everywhere. Dimly lit rooms, one cup of coffee, captions about preferring silence. It looks beautiful.

Psychologists draw a line between solitude and isolation. Isolation is chronic and unchosen. Research consistently identifies it as one of the strongest predictors of early mortality in the literature. Solitude, by contrast, is restorative and temporary. Those are not the same experience, but they can look identical from the outside.

The problem with romanticizing isolation is that the chronic kind becomes harder to name. You cannot easily ask for connection from something you have made your whole identity.
