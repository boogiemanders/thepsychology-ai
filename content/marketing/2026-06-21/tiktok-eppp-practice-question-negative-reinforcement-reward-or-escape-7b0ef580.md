# EPPP practice question: negative reinforcement (reward or escape)

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

E triple P question. A partner starts speaking more quietly at home after their spouse kept leaving the room during arguments. Which process best explains the change in the partner's behavior?

A: Positive reinforcement.
B: Negative reinforcement.
C: Extinction.
D: Classical conditioning.

Most people go straight to A. Behavior changed, something good happened. Feels right. It is not.

The answer is B, negative reinforcement. The partner's quiet speaking increased because the spouse walking out was removed as a consequence. The aversive outcome was removed. Nothing good was added. That is the distinction.

A is wrong because positive reinforcement adds a pleasant stimulus. Nothing pleasant was added here.
C is wrong because extinction means behavior decreases. This behavior increased.
D is wrong because classical conditioning pairs stimuli involuntarily. This is voluntary behavior shaped by consequences.
