# Supervisors are the most disconnected people in the EPPP prep system

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

Supervisors are the most disconnected people in the EPPP prep system. That is not a failure of care. It is a structural gap.

Candidates rarely tell their supervisors how the studying is going. By the time a supervisor finds out a postdoc failed, the next attempt and another prep program cycle is already underway.

California's 2025 overall pass rate was 36.5% (California Board of Psychology, 2026). First-time candidates passed at 58.7%. That means more than 4 in 10 first-timers do not pass.

Three things supervisors can watch for during the prep window:

1. The candidate is studying passively. Reading and re-reading instead of doing practice questions. Active recall is the difference.
2. They are studying all domains equally. Candidates who spread time evenly instead of weighting their weakest areas waste the most hours.
3. They are logging 15 to 20 hours a week and exhausted. More hours does not mean better retention. Six to eight targeted hours outperforms grinding.

None of this is in supervision training. It probably should be.

If a postdoc told you today they have been studying for four months and still feel unprepared, what would you do with that?
