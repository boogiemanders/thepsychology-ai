## Draft

Supervisors are the most confused people in the room when postdocs ask about the 2027 EPPP changes. That is not surprising. The news has been contradictory.

Here is what is actually true, in plain language:

1. The two-part exam is dead. ASPPB paused the EPPP Part 2 skills exam in October 2024 and later abandoned it entirely (California Board of Psychology, 2026, p. 120). If your postdoc is panicking about a skills portion, they can stop.

2. A new integrated exam is being built for fall 2027. ASPPB is now exploring a single-session exam that combines knowledge and skills. No confirmed format yet. Nothing is live.

3. The exam today is unchanged. Your postdoc should study for the current EPPP without alteration. California's first-time pass rate was 58.7% in 2025 (California Board of Psychology, 2026, p. 74). The hard part is the preparation, not the changing rules.

What I tell postdocs in supervision: do not let exam uncertainty change your study plan. Study for what exists today. Adjust when there is something concrete to adjust to.

The panic about 2027 is louder than the actual change.
