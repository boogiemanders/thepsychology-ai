## BUILD-UP (3-8s)

"And before you close the app: no, I'm not here to tell you to delete TikTok. What I want you to know is what the research actually found, because it's more specific and more useful than the headline."
[MIND READ -- clears the assumed payoff; UNEXPECTED TWIST -- reframes what's coming]

## VALUE (8-50s)

"The research is not saying the platform is the problem. The pattern is the problem.

Passive, rapid-fire scrolling with no goal and no stopping point. That's the behavior the research links to increased anxiety and lower mood, especially in younger adults.

The clinical term is passive media consumption. And that is very different from active consumption: watching with intention, for a defined window, then stopping.

If you're a psychologist or a postdoc, this matters twice. Once for your clients. And once for you.

Because the people who will spend careers treating anxiety and digital overwhelm are watching the same feeds."
[EXTREME VALUE -- specific clinical distinction the audience can apply immediately; doubles as a mirror for the professional viewer]

## PAYOFF

"The APA's concern is not TikTok. It's losing an hour without noticing and feeling worse afterward. That's a behavior. And it's one you can actually change."
[PAYOFF -- resolves the hook's contradiction; the irony becomes a practical lesson]
