# The EPPP just changed. Here's what that means for your prep.

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

In October 2024, the ASPPB paused the mandate to implement a two-part EPPP.

The original plan: two separate exams by January 1, 2026, one for knowledge and one for skills. That plan is on hold.

What is replacing it: a single exam that assesses both knowledge and skills together. The structure is still being developed.

For most candidates right now, nothing about your current prep changes. You are still preparing for the 225-question knowledge exam: four hours and fifteen minutes, passing score of 500 in most states.

The exception: DC, Georgia, Guam, and Nevada still require both the knowledge and skills components for licensure. If you are in one of those states, confirm your specific requirements with your board directly.

The bigger picture: the exam format is evolving, but what it tests is not. Clinical reasoning across the 8 domains. Applied knowledge under time pressure. Targeted preparation that addresses your actual gaps.

That stays constant regardless of format changes.
