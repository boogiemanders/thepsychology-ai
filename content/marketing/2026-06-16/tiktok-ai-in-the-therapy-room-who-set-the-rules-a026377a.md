## HOOK (0-3s)
If your therapist is using AI tools, here is what almost nobody is telling patients.

## BUILD-UP (3-8s)
And I'm not saying your therapist is doing something wrong. They probably aren't.

## VALUE (8-35s)
A 2026 study in Frontiers in Psychology found that most professional associations have not issued clear guidance on which AI tools clinicians can or should use. That means the ethical standards for AI in your therapy session are not coming from the A P A or your therapist's licensing board right now. They are coming from the app developers.

There is a psychology concept called automation bias. We naturally give more weight to automated outputs, especially when they come through someone we already trust. When your therapist uses an AI tool, you extend your trust in them to the tool.

The problem is your therapist may be navigating that tool's limitations alone, without official guidance on what is and isn't safe to use.

## PAYOFF
This is not a reason to distrust your therapist. It is a reason to ask them about it.
