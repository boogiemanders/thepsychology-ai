# LinkedIn Outreach Playbook: Postdocs and Doctoral Interns

A hand-run, account-safe way to reach EPPP-bound trainees and get them to try the product free. No bots. No scrapers. No risk to your account.

---

## 1. Why we do this by hand (the honest version)

LinkedIn's User Agreement (Section 8.2) bans every third-party tool that scrapes, auto-connects, or auto-messages. Not just shady scrapers. The "auto-connect" Chrome plugins too. LinkedIn detects these with velocity tracking, message-similarity matching, and browser fingerprinting. In 2025 they ran ban waves and de-platformed tools like Apollo and Seamless. One cited test of 50 automation accounts saw 23% restricted within 90 days. So the risk of the "fast" way was simple: a restricted or permanently dead account, which for a founder is your face and your credibility gone. The slow way (you, logged in, typing at human speed) carries zero restriction risk. That is the single biggest lever, so we pull it. Everything below assumes you, on real linkedin.com or the official app, doing it yourself.

## 2. Sales Navigator search recipes

Sales Nav does not give you more invites (the cap is the same on every plan). It gives you better targeting so your limited invites land on the right people. Three rules before you start:

- Boolean words (`AND` `OR` `NOT`) must be UPPERCASE or LinkedIn treats them as normal words.
- Toggle **Current** on the Job Title filter every time. Default matches past titles too, which surfaces people who already finished training and probably already passed.
- Keep each field under ~15 Boolean operators. If you need more, split into a second saved search.

### Search 1: Postdocs only (your hottest buyers)

These have the doctorate and are accruing hours. The EPPP is the next gate, so intent is highest.

- **Current Job Title:**
```
("Postdoctoral Fellow" OR "Post-Doctoral Fellow" OR "Postdoctoral Resident" OR "Clinical Psychology Resident" OR "Psychology Fellow") NOT (Research OR Biology OR Chemistry OR Engineering OR Medicine OR MD)
```
- **Seniority Level:** Training
- **Industry:** Mental Health Care, Hospital & Health Care, Higher Education
- **Geography:** United States
- Save it.

### Search 2: Interns

- **Current Job Title:**
```
("Predoctoral Intern" OR "Pre-Doctoral Intern" OR "Psychology Intern" OR "Doctoral Intern" OR "Psychology Trainee") AND (Clinical OR Counseling OR "School Psychology" OR Neuropsychology)
```
- **Seniority Level:** Training
- **Industry:** Mental Health Care, Hospital & Health Care, Higher Education
- **Geography:** United States

### Search 3: VA sub-list (clone of Search 1)

The VA is the single largest psychology training employer in the country (~1,264 funded slots, 2021-22). Clone Search 1 and add:

- **Current Company:**
```
"Department of Veterans Affairs" OR "Veterans Affairs" OR "VA Medical Center" OR "VA Healthcare" OR "VHA"
```

### Search 4: Self-declared EPPP studiers (clone, hottest of all)

These people literally announced they are studying. Clone Search 1 and add to **Keywords:**
```
("studying for the EPPP" OR "preparing for the EPPP" OR "EPPP" OR "seeking licensure" OR "licensure-eligible" OR "working toward licensure" OR "postdoctoral hours" OR "supervised hours")
```

### Rank who to message first

1. Title says Postdoctoral Fellow/Resident in psychology (has degree, EPPP next).
2. Profile text says "seeking licensure," "studying for the EPPP," "supervised hours."
3. Education shows PhD/PsyD ending this year or last year.
4. Recent post: "matched," "starting my postdoc," "passed comps," "defended."

Free-search note: if you do not have Sales Nav, the same Boolean strings work in the regular LinkedIn search bar. Targeting is rougher but the message playbook is identical.

## 3. Safe volume and weekly cadence

The cap that matters: **~100 connection requests per week, on every plan.** Paying for Sales Nav does NOT raise it. It is a rolling 7-day window, not a Monday reset.

| Action | Safe limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Connection requests | 15-20/day, 75-100/week | Stay at or below 100. Never spike. |
| Messages to people who accepted | No hard cap, keep under ~100/week | Spread out. |
| Acceptance rate | Keep above 40% | Below 30% is the main warning sign. Slow down if you dip. |
| New/cold account | 14-day low-volume ramp first | Do not go 5 one day, 80 the next. That is an instant flag. |

### Your weekly hand-run rhythm

- **Mon-Fri:** send 15-18 connection requests a day. Spread them across the day, not all in one 9am burst. Mix in normal browsing, a like, maybe a post.
- **Day +1 to +2 after someone accepts:** send Touch 2.
- **Day +5 to +7 after Touch 2 (no reply):** send Touch 3. Then stop. One follow-up only.
- **Weekends:** light or off. Real humans take breaks, so should the account.

If LinkedIn ever shows you a "try again later" message, that is a yellow flag. Stop sending for the day.

## 4. The 3-touch message sequence

Voice rules baked in: short sentences, plain words, no emojis, no em dashes, no hype, peer-to-peer. Never overclaim. Pick the variant that fits the person, then tweak the personalization token.

### Touch 1: Connection request note (under 300 chars, aim under 180)

Data favors restraint. A maxed-out generic note reads as automation and tanks acceptance. So: send a SHORT genuine note when you have a real hook, or send blank when you do not. Do not send templated filler.

**Variant A (postdoc, generic but real):**
> Hi [First], fellow psychologist here. I built an affordable EPPP prep tool after barely surviving the exam myself. Always glad to connect with folks in the thick of postdoc.

**Variant B (shared program / school):**
> Hi [First], saw we both came up through [Program]. I'm a psychologist building EPPP prep for early-career folks. Would be good to connect.

**Variant C (they posted about matching/starting):**
> Hi [First], congrats on starting at [Site]. I'm a psychologist who works with postdocs on EPPP prep. Happy to connect and cheer you on.

**Variant D (self-declared studier):**
> Hi [First], saw you're prepping for the EPPP. I'm a psychologist who passed it and now build prep tools for early-career folks. Connecting in case it's ever useful.

**Variant E (blank):** No note. Send the raw request. Highest acceptance rate. Use when you have no specific, honest hook.

### Touch 2: First message after they accept (value first, soft)

No pitch wall. Lead human, offer help, mention the free trial once, never push.

**Variant A (warm intro, postdoc):**
> Thanks for connecting, [First]. I'm a clinical psychologist (PsyD). I went 19% on my first EPPP diagnostic to a 588 first-attempt pass, and it made me angry how much the big prep programs charge. So I built a cheaper one. If you ever want to try it, it's free for 7 days, no card. No pressure at all, mostly just rooting for you through postdoc.

**Variant B (the realism angle):**
> Thanks for connecting, [First]. Quick context in case it's ever useful: the #1 complaint about EPPP prep is questions that don't sound like the real exam. I built mine to word questions the way the EPPP actually does, application style, not definition recall. There's a free 7-day trial, no credit card, if you ever want to see for yourself.

**Variant C (cost angle, on-brand):**
> Thanks for connecting, [First]. You already carry six figures of training debt, and then the exam is the last toll on top. The big programs run $849 to $1,799. Mine is $40 a month and there's a free week with no card. Sharing in case it saves you some money down the line, not trying to sell you today.

**Variant D (.edu / still-a-student angle):** see Section 6.

### Touch 3: Gentle follow-up (only if no reply, 5-7 days later)

One follow-up. Then leave them alone.

**Variant A:**
> No worries if EPPP season hasn't started for you yet, [First]. I'll leave the link here so it's easy to find when it does: [tracked link]. Good luck with postdoc.

**Variant B:**
> Totally get it, [First], postdoc is a lot. If it's ever useful, the free trial is here with no card needed: [tracked link]. Either way, rooting for you on the exam.

**Variant C (referral-friendly close):**
> All good, [First]. If it's not for you but you know someone in your cohort grinding through EPPP prep, feel free to pass this along: [tracked link]. Cheap and free to try.

Use a tracked link of the form `https://www.thepsychology.ai/go/<unique-slug>`. Drop the link in Touch 3, or in Touch 2 only if the conversation is clearly going somewhere. Do not lead with a link.

## 5. Personalization tokens and rules

Reference ONE real, true thing per message. One is enough and reads human. Three reads like a template.

| Token | Where to find it | Example use |
|---|---|---|
| `[First]` | Their name | Always. |
| `[Program]` | Education section (PhD/PsyD) | "we both came up through Fuller" |
| `[Site]` | Current employer | "congrats on starting at the Boston VA" |
| Near-licensure status | About text, recent posts | Reference "supervised hours," "seeking licensure" only if THEY said it |
| Recent milestone | Their activity feed | "matched," "defended," "passed comps" |

Rules:
- Only reference what is actually on their profile. Guessing wrong kills trust instantly.
- If you have no honest hook, use the blank request (Variant E) and the generic Touch 2.
- Never claim you know their pain better than they do. Side with them, do not lecture.
- Vary your wording across sends. Near-identical copy is exactly what LinkedIn's similarity detection flags.

## 6. The .edu trial and "invite your cohort" angle

**Important:** the extended-.edu-trial and free-months-for-inviting-classmates offer is a NEW campaign idea. Do not promise it until it exists in the product. Once it is live, frame it like this (not as a referral bounty, as a fair-shot-for-your-cohort move):

**Touch 2, .edu variant:**
> Thanks for connecting, [First]. Since you're still on a .edu, you get a longer free run at this, no card. The whole reason I built it cheap: the people most likely to fail the EPPP first try are often the ones who can least afford a retake (Sharpless, 2019). So I'd rather lower the bar to trying. Link's here whenever: [tracked link].

**The cohort invite (frame as mutual aid, never a growth hack):**
> If your study group's all in the same boat, you can invite them and everyone gets free months. Not a referral thing, more just trying to get a whole cohort a fair shot at this without the $849 tax.

Keep the math honest: current price is $40/mo. Trial is 7 days free, no credit card. Do not invent numbers for the .edu extension until product confirms them.

## 7. Tracking sheet

One Google Sheet, one row per person. Lets you watch accept rate and conversion so you know when to slow down or change the message.

| Column | What goes in it |
|---|---|
| Name | Their name |
| Profile URL | Link to their LinkedIn |
| Segment | Postdoc / Intern / VA / EPPP-studier |
| Site / Program | Employer or school (your personalization hook) |
| Touch 1 date | When you sent the request |
| Note variant | A/B/C/D/E (so you can see which note accepts best) |
| Accepted? | Yes / No / Pending |
| Touch 2 date | When you messaged after accept |
| Touch 3 date | Follow-up date (blank if they replied) |
| Replied? | Yes / No |
| Trial started? | Yes / No |
| Notes | Anything human worth remembering |

Two numbers to watch:
- **Accept rate** = Accepted / Touch 1 sent. Keep above 40%. If it drops under 30%, tighten targeting and shorten or drop the note.
- **Trial rate** = Trial started / Replied. This tells you if the message is converting once you're talking.

## 8. Do NOT do this

- No automation tools, ever. No "auto-connect," "auto-message," scraper, or scheduler browser extension. Even the ones marketed as "cloud-based and human-like." All of it violates Section 8.2 and risks a permanent ban.
- No copy-paste blasts. Near-identical messages get flagged by similarity detection and read as spam.
- No buying connections or followers.
- No volume spikes. 15-20 a day, never 5 then 80.
- No maxed-out 300-char sales notes on the connection request. Short and real, or blank.
- No leading with a product link. Links in the request note and early messages tank acceptance and read as a pitch.
- No ignoring the "try again later" warning. That is LinkedIn telling you to stop for the day. Listen to it.
