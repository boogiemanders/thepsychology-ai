# When your patient arrives with a therapy chatbot transcript

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

> [!warning] Needs human review
> Verify NEJM AI Therabot DOI (10.1056/AIoa2400802) and confirm Jacobson as first author and 2024 as publication year before publishing.

## Draft

Patients are showing up to sessions with AI chatbot conversation logs.

Not as a curiosity. A randomized trial published in NEJM AI found that a mental health chatbot produced significant symptom reductions in clinical-level depression and anxiety compared to a control condition (Jacobson et al., 2024). That kind of evidence does not stay in journals.

Two things worth sitting with:

The chatbot is not competing with the therapeutic alliance. It fills the hours between sessions when the alliance is not present. Different function, different window.

A patient who brings you their AI transcript is showing you something. The content of the conversation matters less than the act of bringing it. They are telling you where they have been going for support.

The clinical move is treating it like any other between-session behavior the client discloses: with curiosity, not alarm.

When this comes up in your room, what do you do with it clinically?
