## Draft

Patients Already Did

A randomized trial published in NEJM AI found a mental health chatbot significantly reduced symptoms of clinical-level depression and anxiety.

Your clients have already found that study.

The clinical question is not whether patients are using AI between sessions. They are. The question is what happens when they bring it into the room.

Two things that matter.

First: the chatbot fills the hours between sessions when the alliance is not present. It is not competing with you. Different function, different window.

Second: a client who shows you their chatbot transcript is bringing you clinical material. That is not a red flag. That is therapy.

The clinical move is treating it like any other between-session behavior: with curiosity, not alarm.
