# EPPP Practice Question: Broca's vs. Wernicke's aphasia

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

E triple P aphasia question. UCLA psychologist. Most candidates get this wrong.

There is a trap in this one.

A patient had a stroke. She speaks in short, effortful phrases and drops function words. When she tries to describe walking the dog, she says: dog, outside, walk. Her comprehension is fully intact. She understands you and gets frustrated when the words won't come. Which type of aphasia?

A: Wernicke's aphasia.
B: Global aphasia.
C: Broca's aphasia.
D: Conduction aphasia.

The answer is C, Broca's. Effortful, telegraphic speech with intact comprehension is the signature. She drops function words and keeps content words. Wernicke's area is undamaged, which is why she understands everything.

A is wrong because Wernicke's produces fluent but meaningless speech and comprehension breaks down.
B is wrong because global aphasia destroys both production and comprehension severely.
D is wrong because conduction aphasia preserves fluency but breaks repetition.
