## Draft

California's EPPP first-time pass rate was 58.7 percent in 2025. The overall pass rate was 36.5 percent. The difference between those two numbers is the retake gap (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

Pass rates have been declining year over year since 2021. The exam has not changed.

If you completed the EPPP before 2025, you passed in a different testing environment. The hours you studied, the method you used, the timeline you followed: those benchmarks came from your experience, not from the current numbers.

Postdocs take their cues from supervisors who have been through it. If that advice was formed in a stronger pass-rate environment, it may not fit what candidates are facing now. The retake numbers make this concrete: derived from the 2025 Board data, roughly 1 in 5 retakers passes on a subsequent attempt.

The exam has not changed. The preparation landscape has.
