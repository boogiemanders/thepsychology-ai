# EPPP Social Psych: Hindsight Bias in a Malpractice Jury

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Most people miss this E triple P question about courtroom bias. Let's test you.

A malpractice jury evaluates a psychologist. After learning that a client became violent, the jurors say the warning signs were obvious. A competent clinician should have seen this coming. Which cognitive bias MOST likely explains this judgment?

A. Illusory correlation
B. Hindsight bias
C. Confirmation bias
D. Base rate fallacy

Take a second.

The answer is B, hindsight bias. It is the tendency to believe, after an event occurs, that you could have predicted it. The jurors did not find those warning signs obvious before the violence happened. Knowing the outcome made them look obvious in retrospect. That is the trap.

A is wrong because illusory correlation is seeing relationships that do not actually exist.
C is wrong because confirmation bias is the tendency to seek information that already confirms your beliefs.
D is wrong because base rate fallacy is ignoring statistical rates in favor of specific case details.
