# EPPP CBT: Selective Abstraction

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here is the one CBT concept the E triple P tests every year. Learn this and you gain one clean answer.

A practicum student receives a strongly positive evaluation with one comment: improve your time management. For the rest of the day, the student thinks only about that one comment and concludes their supervisor thinks they are terrible.

Beck calls this thinking error:

A. Personalization
B. Arbitrary inference
C. Selective abstraction
D. Emotional reasoning

The answer is C, selective abstraction. You zoom in on one negative detail and ignore everything else. The whole positive evaluation disappears. One comment becomes the whole verdict.

A is wrong because personalization is taking blame for something outside your control.
B is wrong because arbitrary inference is drawing a conclusion without sufficient evidence. This student has evidence, just a single piece of it.
D is wrong because emotional reasoning uses a feeling as proof of a fact. That is not what is happening here.
