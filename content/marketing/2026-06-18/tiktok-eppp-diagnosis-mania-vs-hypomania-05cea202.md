# EPPP Diagnosis: Mania vs. Hypomania

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here is the one thing that separates mania from hypomania on the E triple P. They mix these up constantly.

Which of these BEST distinguishes a manic from a hypomanic episode?

A. Manic episodes last at least four days and do not cause significant impairment, while hypomanic episodes last at least seven days and cause marked impairment
B. Manic episodes involve irritability only, while hypomanic episodes always require elevated mood
C. Hypomanic episodes require psychotic features, while manic episodes do not
D. Manic episodes last at least seven days and cause marked impairment or hospitalization, while hypomanic episodes last at least four days with no marked impairment and no psychotic features

The answer is D. Mania means at least seven days. Marked impairment or hospitalization. Hypomania means four days minimum. Noticeable change in functioning, but no marked impairment. No psychotic features.

A is wrong because it flips the durations entirely.
B is wrong because both episodes can involve either irritability or elevated mood.
C is wrong because psychotic features belong with mania, not hypomania.
