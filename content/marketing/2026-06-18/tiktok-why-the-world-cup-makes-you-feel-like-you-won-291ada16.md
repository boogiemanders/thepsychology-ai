# Why the World Cup Makes You Feel Like You Won

**TikTok script · Pop Culture**

## Draft

You did not play a single minute. Your country won. And you feel genuinely proud. That is not irrational. That is social identity theory.

Henri Tajfel and John Turner developed this in 1979. The core idea is that part of your self-concept comes from the groups you belong to. When the group wins, the self wins. When the group loses, you feel the threat personally.

Tajfel called this in-group favoritism. We inflate the value of our own groups and downgrade out-groups automatically. It is not conscious. It happens before you decide to feel anything.

The World Cup triggers this at a scale almost nothing else does. Nationality, language, geography, history, all collapse into ninety minutes. The stadium becomes a live experiment in social identity.

This is also why defeats hit harder than victories feel good. Loss threatens group identity. Threat activates defense. Defense looks like blame, anger, or denial.

You are not watching football. You are watching your social self try to survive a result it had no control over.
