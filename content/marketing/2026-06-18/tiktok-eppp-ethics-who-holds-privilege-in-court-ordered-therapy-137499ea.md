# EPPP Ethics: Who Holds Privilege in Court-Ordered Therapy?

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here is the one E triple P ethics question that trips up everyone. The court ordered the therapy. Does that mean the court owns the records?

A judge orders anger management therapy as a condition of probation. The prosecutor subpoenas the therapist's records. Who holds the psychotherapist-client privilege?

A. The client holds the privilege, even in court-ordered therapy
B. The court holds it because it ordered the treatment
C. The therapist holds it independently
D. No privilege exists in court-ordered therapy at all

The answer is A. Privilege always belongs to the client. Not the court. Not the therapist. The court ordered the therapy, it did not take ownership of what is said inside it. Court-ordered evaluations lose privilege. Court-ordered treatment does not.

B is wrong because ordering treatment does not transfer privilege to the court.
C is wrong because therapists can assert privilege on behalf of the client, not as the holder.
D is wrong because court-ordered treatment is categorically different from a court-ordered evaluation.
