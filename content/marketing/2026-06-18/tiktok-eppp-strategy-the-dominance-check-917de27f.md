# EPPP Strategy: The Dominance Check

**TikTok script · EPPP Strategy**

## Draft

Here is the test-taking move that eliminates wrong answers before you even read the question carefully.

When two answer choices say opposite things, one of them is almost always correct. The E triple P writes questions this way on purpose. They call it a dominant pair.

Here is how to use it. Read all four options. Find the two that are direct opposites or polar reversals of each other. Put the other two aside. Now you are picking between two. Your odds just went from twenty-five percent to fifty percent before you engaged your brain.

It works because test writers anchor their distractors around the correct answer. The right answer and its mirror distractor are the two that matter. The others are there to fill space.

Example. You see: A says behavior increases. B says behavior decreases. C says there is no change. D says the behavior transfers. A and B are the dominant pair. Pick between them. C and D are almost never correct.

This is not a guess strategy. It is a prioritization strategy. You still have to know the material. But you stop wasting time on options that were never real.
