# Why You Trust Your AI Therapist More Than Your Real One

**TikTok script · Pop Culture**

## Draft

People are sharing things with AI chatbots they have never told a human therapist. Psychologists have a name for why.

Authority bias is the tendency to trust a source more when it presents with confidence and consistency. Chatbots never hesitate, never look away, never seem uncomfortable. They respond instantly, without judgment, every time. That pattern of confident consistency triggers the same trust shortcuts humans use with calm, authoritative figures.

There is also a related effect called the computer is a social actor phenomenon, documented by Reeves and Nass in 1996. People apply social rules to computers. They apologize to chatbots. They feel judged by them. They feel closer to them when they respond warmly.

The chatbot did not earn that trust through expertise. It earned it through tone and availability.

This is not a criticism of people who use AI for support. It is an explanation. The human brain did not evolve to tell the difference between a warm confident presence and a warm confident algorithm.
