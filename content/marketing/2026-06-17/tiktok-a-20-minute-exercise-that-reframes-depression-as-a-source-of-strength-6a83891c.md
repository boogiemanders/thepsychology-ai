# A 20-minute exercise that reframes depression as a source of strength

**TikTok script · Psychology**

## Draft

A twenty-minute writing exercise increased goal progress by forty-nine percent. That is not a motivation hack. That is a clinical finding.

Published in twenty twenty-six in Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin. Seven hundred forty-eight participants who had experienced depression.

The exercise asks one question: what strengths did you develop by living through depression? Persistence. Emotional depth. Insight into what matters.

Participants went from forty-three percent progress toward a personal goal to sixty-four percent, in just two weeks.

The mechanism is internalized stigma reduction. When you stop seeing depression as evidence of weakness, your capacity to act goes up.
