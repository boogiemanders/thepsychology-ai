# If you supervise a postdoc preparing for the EPPP, three things are worth knowing.

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

If you supervise a postdoc preparing for the EPPP, three things are worth knowing.

In 2025, California's first-time pass rate was 58.7%. Roughly four in ten first-time candidates did not pass (California Board of Psychology, 2026). Most of them had no idea they were at risk.

1. Study method matters more than hours. Retrieval practice predicts outcomes better than re-reading. The research on active recall is unambiguous (Dunlosky, 2013). If your postdoc is re-reading notes, they are using the least efficient method available.

2. Domain weighting is not intuitive. Clinical Psychology carries the most exam weight. Equal time across all eight domains is a common and costly mistake.

3. A high practice test score is not a pass guarantee. Practice exam scores and real exam scores can diverge significantly. The two formats are not interchangeable.

Supervisors are rarely in the room for EPPP conversations. But you are often the one who hears about the outcome.

If a postdoc in your orbit is preparing right now, what do you say when they bring it up?
