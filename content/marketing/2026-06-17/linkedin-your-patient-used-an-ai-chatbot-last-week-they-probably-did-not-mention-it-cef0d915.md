# Your patient used an AI chatbot last week. They probably did not mention it.

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

Your patient used an AI chatbot last week. They probably did not mention it.

The APA's 2026 Chatbots and Mental Health Survey found 35% of psychologists have patients who use AI as an additional mental health professional (American Psychological Association, 2026). Another 39% have patients who used AI to self-diagnose.

That is not a tech story. That is a clinical intake story.

Three practical issues this raises for any working clinician:

1. What you do not ask about in session, you will not know about. AI use is not on most intake forms.

2. AI chatbots are not bound by confidentiality. A patient processing trauma with a chatbot is creating a data record they may not understand.

3. The chatbot is not coordinated with your treatment plan. It may be reinforcing avoidance while you are running exposures.

You do not need to be an AI expert to address this. You need to ask the question.

What is your current approach when a patient discloses AI chatbot use? Do you have a protocol, or is it handled case by case?
