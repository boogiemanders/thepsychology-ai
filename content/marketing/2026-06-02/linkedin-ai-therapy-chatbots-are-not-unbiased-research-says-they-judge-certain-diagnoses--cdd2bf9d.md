# AI therapy chatbots are not unbiased. Research says they judge certain diagnoses more harshly than others.

## Draft

Roughly 1 in 4 adults under 30 have used an AI chatbot for mental health support.

A study from UT Dallas found that these chatbots are not neutral. They showed more stigmatizing language toward schizophrenia and alcohol dependence than toward depression.

This is not surprising if you think about how large language models are trained. They learn from human text. Human text is full of stigma around serious mental illness. The chatbot does not develop bias despite being trained on human data. It develops bias because of it.

What this means for clinicians: AI in mental health is not a neutral referral. An unsupervised chatbot is not a safe first contact for someone presenting with psychosis or a substance use disorder. The tool reflects the culture that produced it.

For the field more broadly, the question is not whether AI belongs in mental health care. It is under what conditions and with what oversight.

"Unbiased" is not a default setting. It is a design choice that requires active effort.
