## Draft

Recent research found that 1 in 3 people have used an AI chatbot for emotional support. Most of them have not discussed it with their therapist.

Here is what the current evidence actually shows.

For mild symptoms, AI chatbots can provide meaningful support. A randomized controlled trial published in NEJM AI found promising results for depression, anxiety, and eating disorder symptoms using a generative AI chatbot. But every study comparing AI chatbots to human therapists reaches the same conclusion: human therapists produce better outcomes, and the gap grows with symptom severity. AI chatbots cannot reliably detect psychiatric emergencies (American Psychological Association).

The challenge is not that clients are using these tools. It is that most are using them without any clinical guidance on what the evidence actually shows.

That is not a technology failure. It is a clinical communication gap.
