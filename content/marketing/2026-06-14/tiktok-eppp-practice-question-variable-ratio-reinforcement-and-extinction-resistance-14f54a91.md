# EPPP practice question: variable-ratio reinforcement and extinction resistance

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

## HOOK (0-3s)
"I'm a UCLA-trained psychologist. Can you answer this E triple P question?"

## BUILD-UP (3-8s)
"This one trips people up."

## VALUE (8-35s)
"A child reinforced on a variable-ratio schedule for completing homework stops getting rewards. What happens next?

A) Immediate extinction.
B) Slow extinction with many responses.
C) Faster extinction than a fixed-ratio schedule.
D) No extinction possible.

A is wrong because variable-ratio schedules are the most extinction-resistant of all reinforcement schedules.
C is wrong because variable-ratio is actually harder to extinguish than fixed-ratio, not easier.
D is wrong because extinction can occur on any schedule, it just takes longer on variable-ratio."

## PAYOFF (35-42s)
"The answer is B. Variable-ratio schedules are the most resistant to extinction. That is why slot machines work. And it is why some of your clients' compulsive behaviors persist long after the reward has disappeared."
