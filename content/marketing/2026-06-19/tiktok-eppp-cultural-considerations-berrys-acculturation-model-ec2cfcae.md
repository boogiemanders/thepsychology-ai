# EPPP Cultural Considerations: Berry's Acculturation Model

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

This is the single easiest way to answer any E triple P question about acculturation. Know Berry's four options.

A recent immigrant keeps her home language with family, maintains her cultural traditions, and also participates fully in the majority culture. Berry's model best describes this as:

A. Assimilation.
B. Integration.
C. Separation.
D. Marginalization.

The answer is B.

Integration means both cultures are active. She keeps the original culture and engages with the new one. Both are running at the same time. That is the definition.

A is wrong because assimilation requires giving up the original culture, and she kept it.
C is wrong because separation means avoiding the majority culture, and she participates in it.
D is wrong because marginalization is disconnection from both cultures, and she is thriving in both.
