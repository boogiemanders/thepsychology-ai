# EPPP Assessment: WAIS-IV Classic Aging Pattern

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here is exactly how you are going to answer any E triple P assessment question about normal cognitive aging. You are going to know the classic pattern.

A neuropsychologist gives the ways four to a cognitively healthy 72-year-old woman. Which index score pattern is most consistent with normal aging?

A. VCI 105, PRI 108, WMI 100, PSI 112.
B. VCI 95, PRI 95, WMI 95, PSI 95.
C. VCI 112, PRI 95, WMI 98, PSI 88.
D. VCI 88, PRI 105, WMI 100, PSI 110.

The answer is C.

Normal aging protects crystallized intelligence and erodes fluid abilities. VCI holds because vocabulary and stored knowledge resist age. Processing speed and fluid reasoning decline because they run on neural efficiency that slows over time. High verbal, lower everything else: that is the healthy brain at 72.

A is wrong because high PSI at 72 contradicts everything we know about aging and processing speed.
B is wrong because flat scores assume aging is equal across all abilities, which it is not.
D is wrong because low VCI with high PRI flips the whole pattern. That is not normal aging.
