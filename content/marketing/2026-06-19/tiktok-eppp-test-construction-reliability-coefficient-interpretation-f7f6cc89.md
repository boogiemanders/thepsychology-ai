# EPPP Test Construction: Reliability Coefficient Interpretation

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here is the ONE thing that will help you answer reliability questions on E triple P.

A test developer reports that a new screening instrument has a reliability coefficient of .84. Which of the following is the best interpretation?

A. Eighty-four percent of the items measure the same construct.
B. There is an eighty-four percent probability that any single obtained score equals the true score.
C. Eighty-four percent of score variability reflects true differences, and sixteen percent reflects random error.
D. The test will correctly classify eighty-four percent of examinees.

The answer is C.

The reliability coefficient tells you how much of the variance in scores comes from real differences between people versus measurement noise. Point 84 means 84 percent is signal, 16 percent is error. That is the classical test theory definition, and the only one that matches here.

A is wrong because internal consistency tells you items correlate with each other, not that they all measure the same construct.
B is wrong because there is no such thing as a probability that a single score equals the true score. That is not how reliability is defined.
D is wrong because classification accuracy is validity, not reliability.
