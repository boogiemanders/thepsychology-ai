# EPPP Research and Stats: Selection Bias in Quasi-Experimental Designs

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Here is the ONE thing to know to stop selection bias from tripping you up on E triple P.

A researcher wants to study the effects of a CBT protocol. Because random assignment is not feasible, she compares clients already enrolled in CBT to a wait-list group. What is the main threat to internal validity?

A. Selection bias, because the groups may differ in ways beyond treatment.
B. Attrition, because participants may drop out at different rates.
C. Instrumentation, because different therapists deliver the protocol.
D. History, because external events may affect both groups differently.

The answer is A.

Quasi-experimental designs skip random assignment. That means your CBT group and your wait-list group probably started different. One group wanted treatment badly enough to seek it. The other did not. That pre-existing difference is selection bias, and it breaks the bridge from sample to any causal conclusion.

B is wrong because attrition is a separate internal validity threat, not this one.
C is wrong because instrumentation is about inconsistent measurement, not group composition.
D is wrong because history affects both groups, so it is a different threat entirely.
