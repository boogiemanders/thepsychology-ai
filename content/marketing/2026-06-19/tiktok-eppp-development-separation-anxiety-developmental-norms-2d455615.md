# EPPP Development: Separation Anxiety Developmental Norms

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

Today we are talking about how to stop guessing on E triple P developmental questions. Know the norms.

A pediatrician refers a 16-month-old because the child becomes extremely distressed whenever the caregiver leaves the room. Which response is most accurate?

A. A sign of insecure-resistant attachment requiring immediate intervention.
B. An early indicator of an anxiety disorder, since separation anxiety normally resolves by 12 months.
C. Atypical, because separation anxiety does not normally emerge until 18 to 24 months.
D. Developmentally appropriate, since separation anxiety peaks between 14 and 18 months.

The answer is D.

Separation anxiety follows a predictable arc. It emerges around 6 to 8 months and peaks between 14 and 18 months. A 16-month-old screaming when a parent leaves is not a symptom. It is a milestone.

A is wrong because distress on its own does not indicate insecure attachment. The pediatrician just met this kid.
B is wrong because separation anxiety does not resolve by 12 months. It peaks after that.
C is wrong because separation anxiety has been present for months at this point, not just emerging.
