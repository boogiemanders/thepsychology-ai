## Draft

If you have been researching the EPPP and came across references to "EPPP Part 2," "EPPP-2," or the "integrated exam," you are right to be confused. The plan has changed multiple times since 2022. This post explains what actually happened, where things stand as of mid-2026, and what the EPPP integrated exam 2027 timeline means for you right now.

The short version: EPPP Part 2 was paused, then abandoned. The exam you need to pass today is unchanged. A new structure is being discussed for 2027, but nothing is confirmed.

---

## What Was EPPP Part 2?

ASPPB, the organization that administers the EPPP, spent years developing a second component called the EPPP Part 2 Skills Exam, also referred to as the EPPP-2 or the Integrated Examination for Professional Practice in Psychology. The Part 2 was designed to assess applied professional skills through a separate testing session, rather than the multiple-choice knowledge format of the current exam.

In May 2023, the California Board of Psychology approved rules to add a Part 2 requirement to California licensure, with an implementation date of January 1, 2026 (California Board of Psychology, 2026). Other states were expected to follow similar timelines.

---

## What Happened: The Pause and the Abandonment

In October 2024, ASPPB paused the plan to make the EPPP a mandatory two-part exam. The California Board of Psychology paused its related rule work at the same time (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

ASPPB subsequently abandoned the two-part structure entirely. The California Board's February 2026 meeting materials describe this as an abrupt decision that forced the Board to revisit rules it had already approved (California Board of Psychology, 2026). The Part 2 that was supposed to launch January 1, 2026 never launched. The requirement was dropped before a single candidate was required to take it.

For anyone who delayed testing because they expected a two-part exam: that delay cost you time on a requirement that no longer exists.

---

## Where Things Stand Now: The EPPP Integrated Exam 2027

With the two-part plan abandoned, ASPPB is now signaling a different approach. Early indications from the organization suggest they are exploring a single-session integrated exam that combines knowledge and skills assessment rather than separating them into two parts. The California Board's February 2026 meeting materials note that 2027 is the earliest possible implementation window and that Board members were discussing what a 2027 structure might look like (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

Important caveat: no final format, scoring criteria, or state-level adoption requirements for any 2027 exam have been confirmed. ASPPB has described their work as an exploration of feasibility. States like California would each need to approve new regulations before any new structure applies to their candidates. That regulatory process typically takes a year or more.

The 2027 window is a date to watch, not a deadline to plan around.

---

## The Eligibility Change Running Parallel to This

Separate from the exam format question, there is an eligibility question still being resolved.

Under current California rules, candidates can test for the EPPP after finishing all required coursework, even before completing their post-doctoral hours. ASPPB's original EPPP2 framework proposed tying exam eligibility to the completion of post-doctoral hours, not just coursework (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

California's AB 282 moved to let candidates test after finishing all coursework, and the California Psychological Association has supported the position that states, not ASPPB, should set their own eligibility rules (California Board of Psychology, 2026). That policy question was still unresolved as of February 2026.

This matters because if eligibility rules change, future candidates may face a longer wait before they can sit for the exam, regardless of what the exam itself looks like.

## Why This History Matters for You

The EPPP2 saga is not just a policy footnote. It directly affected how thousands of candidates planned their timelines.

Some candidates delayed testing, expecting to take a Part 2. Others altered their study timelines based on rumored changes to the format. A few held off on California licensure entirely, waiting to see how the rules settled.

The lesson from the past two years: exam policy announcements are not the same as exam requirements. A rule approved by a licensing board can be paused, amended, or abandoned before it takes effect. The only exam you are required to pass is the one that is currently law.

For context on why passing that exam is urgent, California's first-time pass rate was 58.7% in 2025, lower than in each of the prior four years (California Board of Psychology, 2026). The current EPPP is harder to pass than many candidates expect. Waiting for a change that has not been confirmed is not a strategy.

## What to Do If You Are Studying Right Now

**Study for the exam that exists.** The EPPP is a 225-question multiple-choice exam covering eight content domains. That is what is required today, and it is what any future integrated structure would build from. Time spent on the current domains is not wasted regardless of what 2027 brings.

**Do not shift your timeline based on the 2027 window.** Even if ASPPB finalizes an integrated exam structure for 2027, California would need to adopt it through a separate regulatory process. That timeline is not guaranteed. Candidates currently eligible to test should test on the current exam.

**Watch two sources for updates.** If something significant changes, it will appear in ASPPB's official communications and in California Board of Psychology meeting materials, which are posted publicly at psychology.ca.gov. Social media speculation and secondhand summaries are not reliable primary sources.

**Do not let the noise change how you prep.** The core competencies tested on the EPPP, clinical psychology, biological bases, research methods, ethics, assessment, and the rest, are unlikely to change fundamentally even if the format shifts. Deep content knowledge is the right foundation regardless.

If you are ready to start building that foundation, [How to Pass the EPPP on Your First Try](/blog/how-to-pass-the-eppp-first-try) walks through the methodology that works for the exam as it currently stands.

## The Bottom Line

The EPPP Part 2 was developed, approved by states including California, paused, and then abandoned. A new EPPP integrated exam is being discussed for 2027, but the specific structure and state-level adoption are not confirmed.

The exam waiting for you right now is the same one that has existed for decades. It is hard. The pass rate is trending down. Starting your preparation now, on the current exam, is the only move that makes sense.
