# A generative AI chatbot just passed an RCT for clinical depression. Here is what it says.

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

A fully generative AI chatbot showed significant symptom reduction in a randomized controlled trial for clinical-level depression, generalized anxiety, and eating disorders.

The chatbot is Therabot, developed at Dartmouth. Published in NEJM AI (NEJM AI, 2024).

Here is what the study says, and what it does not say.

It says: a supervised, expert-fine-tuned AI tool can produce measurable symptom change in some populations under controlled conditions with active clinical monitoring.

It does not say: it matches the efficacy of therapy. The researchers built safeguards in and actively monitored every participant.

The useful question for us is not whether this replaces therapy. It does not. The question is: what do you say to the client who spent six months with an AI instead of calling your office? Because that client is probably already in your caseload.

Research now confirms that many people turn to AI with their emotional struggles before contacting a clinician (WBUR, 2026). That is not a future scenario.

How are you handling it when a client comes in having used an AI between sessions?
