# 2026 Is the New 2016

**TikTok script · Pop Culture**

## Draft

**HOOK**
Why is everyone suddenly convinced 2016 was the greatest year of their life?

**BUILD-UP**
There is a clinical reason for this, and knowing it will change how you think about your own memories.

**VALUE**
It is called fading affect bias. Negative emotions attached to memories fade faster than positive ones. So when you look back at 2016, the stress, the awkward moments, the hard parts? Gone. The good parts stay vivid.

This is not just nostalgia. Nostalgia is the emotional pull toward the past. Fading affect bias is the memory distortion that makes the past look better than it actually was.

2016 was not paradise. Your brain has just softened the difficult memories while keeping the positive ones clear.

Psychologists call applying this to whole eras rosy retrospection. It spikes during periods of uncertainty, which explains a lot about right now.

**PAYOFF**
Your brain is not broken. It is doing exactly what it evolved to do.
