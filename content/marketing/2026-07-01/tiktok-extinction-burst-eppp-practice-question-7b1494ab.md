# Extinction Burst — EPPP Practice Question

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

[HOOK]
You remove the reward. The behavior explodes. What is happening?

[BUILD-UP]
And before you say punishment, no. This is one of the most common traps on E triple P.

[QUESTION]
A mother gives her child a snack every time they cry. She stops. The next two days, the child cries louder and more often. What explains this?

A — spontaneous recovery. B — negative reinforcement. C — extinction burst. D — stimulus generalization.

[VALUE]
A is wrong because spontaneous recovery means a conditioned response returns after rest. Not this situation.

B is wrong because negative reinforcement removes something aversive to increase a behavior. Wrong direction.

D is wrong because stimulus generalization requires responding to similar stimuli. There is no new stimulus here.

[PAYOFF]
The answer is C. Extinction burst. When you remove reinforcement, the behavior intensifies before it stops. The child is not getting worse. The behavior is at its peak right before it dies.
