# The first RCT of a generative AI therapy chatbot is out. The results tell both sides.

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

The first randomized controlled trial of a generative AI therapy chatbot was just published in NEJM AI. The results tell both sides of the story.

The study: 210 adults with clinically significant depression, anxiety, or eating disorder risk. Four weeks. Randomized against a waitlist control (Heinz et al., 2025).

What Therabot produced:
- Significant symptom reductions across all three conditions
- 95% of participants actually engaged with it
- Positive therapeutic alliance ratings

That is a meaningful clinical finding for a chatbot.

Here is what the researchers said directly: no generative AI agent is ready to operate fully autonomously in mental health. Too many high-risk scenarios (Heinz et al., 2025).

The map this gives us: AI can provide real front-line support for lower-acuity symptom management. For complex presentations, crisis situations, and anything requiring live clinical judgment, human care still has no equivalent.

The psychologist's role is not shrinking. It is becoming more specific.
