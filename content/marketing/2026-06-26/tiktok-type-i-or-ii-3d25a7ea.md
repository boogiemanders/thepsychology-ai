# Type I Or II?

**TikTok script · EPPP**

## Draft

HOOK (0-3s):
Most E triple P candidates get this question right for the wrong reason.

BUILD-UP (3-8s):
Which means a version they have not seen before trips them up on test day. Here is the question.

VALUE (8-40s):
A researcher compares two treatments. No significant difference is found between them. P equals point-zero-seven. Which error might have occurred?

A is wrong because Type I is a false positive. You rejected a null hypothesis that was actually true. You found a difference that was not real.

The correct answer is B: Type II error. It is a false negative. The study missed a real effect, probably because the sample was too small.

PAYOFF:
Whenever you see no significant difference paired with a small sample or borderline p-value, think Type II. The study lacked the power to detect what was there.
