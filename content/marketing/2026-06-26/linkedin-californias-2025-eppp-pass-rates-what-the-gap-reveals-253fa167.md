# California's 2025 EPPP pass rates: what the gap reveals

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

California's 2025 EPPP overall pass rate is 36.5%.

That number is lower than most candidates realize, and there is a reason for it.

The overall rate includes everyone who sits, repeat takers and first-timers together. The first-time pass rate is 58.7% (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

That gap matters. It means repeat takers are pulling the overall number down, which tells you something: the method that failed the first time is often being used again.

2025 pass rates were also trending lower than 2021 through 2024. The exam is not getting easier, and grinding harder on the same approach will not fix a method problem.

Two things fail candidates on retakes: real content gaps in 2 to 3 domains, and a flawed study approach. Content gaps are fixable in 6 to 8 focused weeks. Method problems persist indefinitely if left undiagnosed.
