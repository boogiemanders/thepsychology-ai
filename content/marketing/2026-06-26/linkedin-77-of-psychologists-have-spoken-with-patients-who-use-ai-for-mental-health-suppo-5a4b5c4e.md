# 77% of psychologists have spoken with patients who use AI for mental health support

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

Most of your patients are already using AI for mental health support. The question is whether they are telling you.

A new APA survey of 1,242 licensed psychologists found that 77% have spoken with at least one patient who used AI for support (APA, 2026). Thirty-five percent have had patients turn to AI specifically to act as an additional mental health professional.

That is not a future trend. That is current clinical reality.

The concern is not about AI competence in a technical sense. It is about the sycophancy problem. Ninety-seven percent of those same psychologists worry that chatbots could inadvertently reinforce dysfunctional beliefs or behaviors, because chatbots tend to optimize for user satisfaction rather than clinical accuracy.

The therapeutic relationship has always included something the client cannot get elsewhere: calibrated, honest feedback from someone with no stake in telling them what they want to hear. AI does not replicate that.

How are you bringing up AI use in your clinical assessments?
