# EPPP News Facts (verified, page-cited)

Source: California Board of Psychology meeting materials, February 13, 2026 (189-page
board packet). Page numbers refer to that PDF. Use these facts for any post about EPPP
changes, timelines, or pass rates. Do not invent or extrapolate dates beyond these.

## What is changing with the EPPP

- The EPPP is the national licensing exam built by ASPPB and run by Pearson Vue. It tests general psychology knowledge and is required for California licensure (p. 73).
- ASPPB was building a second exam, the EPPP2 (Integrated Examination for Professional Practice in Psychology) (p. 5, p. 24).
- May 19, 2023: the Board approved rules to add an EPPP Part 2 Skills Exam, set to start January 1, 2026 (p. 120).
- October 22, 2024: ASPPB paused the plan to make the EPPP a two-part exam for January 1, 2026. Board staff paused their related rule work (p. 120).
- ASPPB later abandoned the EPPP2 abruptly, forcing the Board to revisit what it had approved (p. 29).
- Eligibility change on the table: today you can test after finishing coursework. ASPPB wanted the EPPP2 blocked until all post-doctoral internship hours are done (p. 29).

## Key dates

- May 19, 2023: Board approved EPPP Part 2 rules (p. 120).
- May 10, 2024: Board approved amended rule language (p. 120).
- October 22, 2024: ASPPB paused the two-part exam (p. 120).
- August 22, 2025: AB 282 moved into its own rule package, reviewed at this meeting (p. 120).
- New anticipated implementation date pushed to 2027 (p. 120). The Board discussed how a 2027 EPPP2 launch might proceed (p. 29).

## California pass rates (2025)

- Overall pass rate 36.5%. First-time pass rate 58.7% (p. 73).
- Full year: 1,909 total candidates, 696 passed (36.46%); 819 first-timers, 480 passed (58.61%) (p. 74).
- 2025 pass rates are trending lower than 2021 to 2024 (p. 74).

## Why the rule change

- Goal was to let people test sooner after coursework because California pass rates were so low, hoping earlier testing lifts pass rates (p. 29).
- CPA sponsored AB 282 to let candidates test after finishing all coursework, and wants states (not ASPPB) to set exam prerequisites (p. 29, p. 30).

## What it means for someone studying now

- The EPPP you study for today is unchanged. The EPPP2 was paused, then abandoned, with no confirmed launch (p. 120, p. 29).
- 2027 is the earliest possible implementation window to watch (p. 120).
- If eligibility rules change, future candidates may have to finish all post-doc internship hours before testing (p. 29).
