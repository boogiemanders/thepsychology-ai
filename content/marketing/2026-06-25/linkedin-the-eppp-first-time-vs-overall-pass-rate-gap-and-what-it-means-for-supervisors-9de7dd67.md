# The EPPP first-time vs overall pass rate gap, and what it means for supervisors

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

California's EPPP first-time pass rate in 2025 was 58.7%. The overall rate was 36.5%.

That gap is the whole story.

First-time candidates pass at nearly 59%. Overall candidates pass at 36%. Most failures are repeat takers. A significant portion of candidates who fail once go on to fail again.

Every retake is more fees, more months of delayed licensure, more income and career momentum lost.

This data is from the California Board of Psychology's February 2026 meeting materials (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

Three things supervising psychologists can tell postdocs before they sit for the first time:

1. Take a diagnostic before you start studying, not after. Know your domain gaps first.
2. Practice questions over passive reading. The EPPP tests application, not recall of definitions.
3. First attempt outcomes matter. The numbers are meaningfully better on attempt one.

When do you recommend your postdocs sit, and how do you prepare them?
