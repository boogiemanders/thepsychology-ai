# What clinicians need to know about patients using AI for mental health support

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

One in three psychologists now has a patient using AI for mental health support between sessions.

Not as a mood tracker. As a provider substitute.

That figure comes from a 2026 American Psychological Association survey of practicing psychologists.

The clinical implication is not about the chatbot. It is about what patients are telling the chatbot that they are not telling you.

The chatbot has no confidentiality obligation, no ability to detect a psychiatric emergency, and no clinical judgment. Most training programs have no protocol for how to address it in treatment. There is no APA practice guideline yet.

Clinicians are being asked to form a clinical stance on something in real time, with no consensus framework, while patients are already mid-treatment with these tools.

Have you had a patient bring up AI use in session? How do you handle that clinically?
