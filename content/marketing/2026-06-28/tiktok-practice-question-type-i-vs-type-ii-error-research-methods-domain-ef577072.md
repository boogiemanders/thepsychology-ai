On the E triple P, two answer choices can look almost identical. One word in the stem separates them. Here is one.
QUESTION
A researcher sets alpha at point oh five. She gets p equals point oh three and rejects the null. A year later, replications prove the null was actually true. What type of error did she commit?
A. Type II error
B. Beta error
C. Power failure
D. Type I error

Pause. What do you think?

PAYOFF
The answer is D, Type I error. She rejected a null that was actually true. That is the definition of Type I. Alpha is the probability of making this exact mistake.
B is wrong because beta is the probability of Type II, not Type I. A is wrong because Type II is the opposite: failing to reject a false null. C is wrong because low power increases Type II risk, not Type I.
