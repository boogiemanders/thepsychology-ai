# Clinicians are the most cautious group about AI in mental health. That is not ignorance.

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

Clinicians are the most cautious group about AI in mental health. That is not ignorance.

The APA surveyed 1,200 licensed psychologists in April 2026. 77% say patients are already using AI for mental health support. 94% say today's chatbots cannot treat mental health conditions with sufficient nuance (APA, 2026).

The concern that matters: 39% have had patients use AI to self-diagnose. 89% say chatbots may inadvertently encourage self-harm.

That is not a case against AI in clinical work. It is a case for clinical judgment about how and when, which is exactly what the profession is built for.

The clinicians least prepared for this shift are not the skeptics. They are the ones who have not yet been asked about it.

When a patient brings up their AI app in session, what is your first move?
