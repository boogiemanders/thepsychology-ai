# If you supervise a postdoc, the exam they will take is not the one you passed.

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

If you supervise a postdoc, the exam they will take is not the one you passed.

In February 2026, ASPPB announced the content specifications for a new integrated EPPP. Beta administration: spring 2027. Operational: fall 2027 (ASPPB, 2026).

What changes: the current content areas are reorganized into six domains. The exam adds audio and video question formats, assesses applied skills alongside factual knowledge, and is planned to run about five hours.

For anyone testing in 2026 or early 2027, the current exam is unchanged.

For a postdoc two years from testing, the ground is shifting.

Most supervisors are not aware of this yet. Most postdocs are not either.

What do you tell a supervisee who asks whether they should rush to test before the new format goes live?
