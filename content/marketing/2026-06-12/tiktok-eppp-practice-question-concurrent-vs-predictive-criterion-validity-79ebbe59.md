## VALUE [7-37s]
A psychologist uses a depression inventory. Scores correlate strongly with therapist ratings of depression severity, also collected that same week. Which type of validity does this demonstrate?

A: face validity. B: construct validity. C: concurrent criterion validity. D: predictive criterion validity.

Criterion validity means the test correlates with an external benchmark. The two subtypes split on timing. Concurrent means both were measured at the same time. Predictive means the criterion is measured in the future.

## PAYOFF [37-43s]
Both were measured the same week. The answer is C.

The trap is B. Construct validity asks whether the test measures the underlying theoretical construct. This question never tests that. It just shows two measures agree right now. Concurrent, not construct.
