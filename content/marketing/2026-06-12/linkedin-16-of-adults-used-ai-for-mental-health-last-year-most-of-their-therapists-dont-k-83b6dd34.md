# 16% of adults used AI for mental health last year. Most of their therapists don't know.

**LinkedIn post · Psychology + AI**

## Draft

16% of US adults used AI tools for mental health in the past year. Among teenagers, 28% message chatbots daily (Jolicoeur, 2026).

Most of their therapists have no idea.

Dr. John Torous, a psychiatrist at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, described it plainly: "At the end of the day, it's a piece of computer code. It has no empathy."

That is the clinical tension. Chatbots feel warm because they reflect your words back in validating language. That reflection can be useful between sessions. But it is pattern matching, not presence.

One clinical social worker described clients sharing his therapeutic observations with an AI and getting responses that "end up sort of invalidating the work." That is a real clinical conversation worth having.

Practical step: start asking. "Have you used any apps or AI tools between sessions?" You will be surprised how many say yes, and what they say the AI told them.
