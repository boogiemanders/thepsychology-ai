# California's EPPP pass rate gap is wider than most candidates realize

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

In California, the first-time EPPP pass rate in 2025 was 58.7%. The overall pass rate was 36.5% (California Board of Psychology, 2026).

That 22-point gap is not noise. It tells a clear story.

Out of 1,909 total candidates, 696 passed. First-timers did meaningfully better: 480 out of 819 passed. The overall number is pulled down because retakers are struggling.

Most retakers do not change their method. They add hours to the same approach that already failed them.

The fix is not more studying. It is a different kind:

- Take a diagnostic before you study anything else.
- Rank domains by exam weight and your current weakness, not by chapter order.
- Use practice questions as the primary activity, not textbook reading.
- Protect 6-8 focused hours per week over passive grinding at 15-20.

First-time pass is not lucky. It is methodological.
