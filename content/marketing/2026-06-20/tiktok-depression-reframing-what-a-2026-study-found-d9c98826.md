# Depression Reframing: What a 2026 Study Found

**TikTok script · Psychology**

## Draft

## HOOK
What if your history of depression made you better at reaching your goals?

## BUILD-UP
A 2026 study tested this. Seven hundred and forty-eight participants across three experiments.

## VALUE
Psychologists built a 20-minute writing exercise. Short stories showing that people who have faced depression develop real strengths: persistence, self-awareness, the capacity to push through hard things. Then participants reflected on how those strengths applied to their own goals. Two weeks later, goal progress was 49 percent higher in the exercise group than the control group.

## PAYOFF
The researchers called it depression reframing. Not toxic positivity. The recognition that struggle leaves something real behind. That residue can move you forward.
