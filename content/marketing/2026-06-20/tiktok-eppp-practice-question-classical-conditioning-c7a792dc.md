## BUILD-UP
This is about classical conditioning. The tricky part is knowing what the stimulus becomes after learning happens.

## QUESTION
A client says hospital antiseptic used to mean nothing to her. Now every time she smells it, she feels anxious. In classical conditioning terms, what is the antiseptic smell called?

A: unconditioned stimulus
B: conditioned stimulus
C: conditioned response
D: neutral stimulus

## ANSWER
The answer is B: conditioned stimulus. The smell learned to trigger anxiety through repeated pairing with the hospital trauma. A is wrong because the unconditioned stimulus is what originally caused the fear. C is wrong because the conditioned response is the anxiety itself. D is wrong because neutral stimulus is what it was before conditioning.
