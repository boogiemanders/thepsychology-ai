## Draft

[HOOK]
Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce are getting married on June 13th. Millions of people who have never met them are going to feel something real about that.

[BEAT: scroll through social media visual]
That feeling has a name. It is called a parasocial relationship.

Parasocial relationships are one-sided bonds that feel psychologically genuine. You know them. They do not know you. But your brain does not fully register that gap.

[BEAT: face cam, explanatory tone]
The reason is evolutionary. For most of human history, the only people you saw and heard regularly were people who also knew you. Your brain evolved to treat familiarity as relationship.

Celebrity media collapsed that assumption. You can have years of exposure to someone who will never know your name. The brain processes that familiarity the same way it processes real connection, because it never had to distinguish the two before.

[BEAT: shift tone, measured]
Parasocial relationships are not inherently harmful. Research shows they can provide a genuine sense of social belonging, particularly for people who feel isolated.

But they are asymmetric. The celebrity does not grow with you. The relationship never asks anything of you. Real relationships do.

[BEAT: CTA, warm and direct]
Enjoy the wedding. Feel what you feel. Parasocial connection is real in one direction.

Just make sure the other direction has people in it.
