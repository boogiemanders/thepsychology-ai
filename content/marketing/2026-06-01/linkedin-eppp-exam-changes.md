# The EPPP is changing again. What candidates need to know right now.

**LinkedIn post · EPPP**

## Draft

The ASPPB just paused its plan to split the EPPP into two separate exams.

For most of 2025, candidates were told they would need to pass both Part 1 (Knowledge) and a new Part 2 (Skills) exam. The original deadline was January 1, 2026.

That plan is now on hold.

The ASPPB is exploring a single-session exam that integrates both knowledge and skills assessments. Town halls and community feedback sessions are ongoing. Only four jurisdictions (DC, Georgia, Guam, and Nevada) currently require both parts for licensure.

What this means practically:

Part 1 (Knowledge) is still your primary target. That has not changed.

But the exam is changing is not a reason to delay. Candidates who wait for clarity on exam structure are still waiting when their window closes. Study the content. Use practice questions. Focus on your weakest domains. Those fundamentals hold regardless of what the ASPPB decides.

If you are in DC, Georgia, Guam, or Nevada, check your state board requirements directly. You may still face a Part 2 requirement.
