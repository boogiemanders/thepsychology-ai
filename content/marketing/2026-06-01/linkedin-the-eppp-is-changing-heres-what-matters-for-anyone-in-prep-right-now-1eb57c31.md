## Draft

The EPPP is changing. Here is what matters if you are in prep right now.

ASPPB paused the two-part exam mandate in late 2024. Instead of a separate knowledge test and skills test, they are building a single Integrated EPPP that combines both in one exam. The current target is Q4 2027.

What this means today:

If you are testing in 2026: nothing changes. Same 225 multiple-choice questions, same four-hour-and-fifteen-minute time limit, same 500-point passing threshold in most states and provinces.

If you are planning to test in late 2027 or beyond: watch ASPPB announcements closely. The format is evolving.

The deeper implication: a single integrated format will reward applied judgment over pure content recall. That is a more honest test of clinical readiness. It also makes passive study methods even less sufficient than they already are.

Prepare for the exam in front of you. Stay informed on what is coming.

Anyone else tracking the 2027 integrated exam timeline? What are you hearing from your programs?
