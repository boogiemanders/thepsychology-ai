[Beat 1]
Labubu is June's most viral collectible. It is a character created by Hong Kong artist Kasing Lung. Viral unboxing videos. Resale markets. People building shrines to a small figure that looks, by most standards, a little off.

And a lot of people's first reaction is: I don't get it.

[Beat 2 -- the psychology]
That confusion is the point. This is social identity theory working on you in real time.

You either get Labubu or you don't. That gap creates an in-group. Belonging to the in-group feels good because the brain reads "I appreciate something others don't understand" as a form of discernment and social status.

This is the same mechanism behind every acquired taste, from experimental jazz to bitter coffee. Liking something unconventional signals judgment, not just trend-following.

[Beat 3]
Every collectibles craze runs on this formula: in-group belonging plus perceived scarcity. The brain rewards both signals at the same time, and the result is a strong pull that feels almost irrational from the outside.
