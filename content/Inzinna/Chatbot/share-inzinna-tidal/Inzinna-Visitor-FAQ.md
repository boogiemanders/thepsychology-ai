# What we offer {#what-we-offer}

## Individual therapy {#individual-therapy}

We offer one-on-one psychotherapy for adults, teens, and kids. We work on depression, anxiety, trauma, relationship stress, identity, grief, life transitions, ADHD, and more. Our approach is collaborative and evidence-based, drawing on CBT, DBT, psychodynamic therapy, and trauma-focused work depending on what fits you best.

## Couples and family therapy {#couples-and-family-therapy}

We help couples and families improve communication, work through conflict, and navigate change. Our clinicians draw from Gottman, Emotionally Focused Therapy, Structural Family Therapy, and Object Relations approaches.

## Child and adolescent therapy {#child-and-adolescent-therapy}

We see kids and teens for anxiety, depression, school stress, behavioral challenges, ADHD, trauma, and family conflict. For younger kids we use play therapy. For older kids and teens we use age-appropriate CBT and family-involved work. Parents are part of the process.

## Group therapy and skills training {#group-therapy-and-skills-training}

We periodically offer groups around shared concerns: anxiety management, social skills, DBT-informed coping skills. Availability varies. Ask us what is currently running.

## Psychological assessment {#psychological-assessment}

We do diagnostic and educational evaluations for kids, teens, and adults. Common reasons people request an assessment: ADHD, learning differences, emotional functioning, treatment planning. Reports are written to be clear and useful, not jargon-heavy.

# Who we work with {#who-we-work-with}

We see adults, teens, kids, couples, and families. Most of our work is outpatient psychotherapy and assessment. We work with a wide range of presenting concerns, and we are equipped to support some higher-acuity cases (non-life-threatening eating disorders, self-harm, substance use, certain psychotic disorders) when they fall within our clinical scope and supervision structure.

# What we cannot treat {#what-we-cannot-treat}

We are an outpatient practice. We are not the right fit if you need:

* Active crisis stabilization or 24-hour care.
* Inpatient psychiatric care.
* Severe or unstable psychotic disorders.
* Life-threatening eating disorders requiring medical stabilization.
* Severe substance dependence requiring detox or rehab.
* Acute suicidal or homicidal risk.

If any of those apply, we will help you find the right level of care. If you are in immediate danger, call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. You can also call or text 988, the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline.

# Telehealth {#telehealth}

We use SimplePractice, a HIPAA-compliant telehealth platform. After you book, you will get a secure session link by email. You join the session through your browser. No app download required.

A few things to know:

* Telehealth is only available if you are physically in New York State at the time of the session. This is a licensure rule.
* You need a stable internet connection and a private space.
* Headphones are recommended, especially if the session covers sensitive topics.
* If the connection drops, your clinician will try to reconnect for up to 10 minutes, then may switch to a phone call.

Telehealth is not appropriate for everyone. If you are in active crisis or do not have a private space, we may recommend in-person care.

# What to expect {#what-to-expect}

## First session {#first-session}

The first session is mostly about getting to know you. Your clinician will ask about what brought you in, your history, and what you are hoping to work on. You can ask any questions you have about the process, fit, or logistics. There is no pressure to share everything in the first session. You set the pace.

## Session length and frequency {#session-length-and-frequency}

Therapy sessions are typically [SESSION_LENGTH] long. Most clients meet weekly to start, then move to biweekly or monthly as things stabilize. Frequency is something you and your clinician decide together.

## How long does therapy take {#how-long-does-therapy-take}

It depends on what you are working on. Some clients meet for a few months around a specific issue. Others stay in longer-term work. Your clinician will check in with you regularly about goals and progress.

# Cancellation policy {#cancellation-policy}

We ask for at least 48 hours notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. Sessions canceled or missed under 48 hours notice are billed a $75 fee. Insurance does not cover missed sessions, so this fee is billed directly to you.

Exceptions may be made for emergencies, sudden illness, or circumstances beyond your control. Just let us know.

# Privacy and confidentiality {#privacy-and-confidentiality}

What you share in therapy is confidential and protected by HIPAA. There are a few legal exceptions where we are required to break confidentiality:

* If we believe you are in imminent danger of harming yourself or someone else.
* If we have reason to suspect child abuse, elder abuse, or abuse of a vulnerable adult.
* If we are subpoenaed by a court.

Your clinician will go over confidentiality and its limits with you at the first session.
