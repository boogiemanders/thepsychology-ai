Where is the brand today?

 Inzinna lives inside a system under pressure.

 Demand for therapy is surging.
 Clinicians are stretched.
 Insurance expands access but tightens margins.
 Private pay expands income but excludes too many people.

 The category isn’t completely broken. It’s wildly inconsistent.

 Some care is excellent. Some is ineffective.
 Most people can’t tell the difference until they’ve already paid.




                                                 Complete Health // AuthorityInsite℠ // 7 April 2025
Where is the brand today?

Consumers show up because something in their life stopped working.

They feel stuck. Overwhelmed. Misunderstood.
Quietly broken in ways they can’t explain.

They’ve tried talking to friends, apps, self diagnosis and self-help books.
And therapists who “weren’t a ﬁt.”

They’re skeptical.

Cost matters. Time matters. Emotional risk matters.

They want someone who actually listens. Someone they can trust.
Something that helps. Fewer wrong turns.




                                                Complete Health // AuthorityInsite℠ // 7 April 2025
Where is the brand today?

Clinicians live under a different kind of pressure.

Long training. Inconsistent supervision. Private-pay tradeoffs.
Platform models that offer volume but little backing.

Trainees work for free or close to it. New clinicians are left on their own fast.
Experienced ones burn out or narrow their caseloads to survive.

Most end up choosing between income, isolation or compromise.

Most didn’t get into the ﬁeld to run a solo business.

They got into it to help people.

Where is the brand today?

That’s the world you work in.

You experienced it all. And called it not good enough.

Your experiences have shaped an Inzinna vision.
One of access instead of exclusion. Support instead of sink or swim.
Virtual delivery as reach, not convenience.
And always in-the-moment presence and honesty at the core of the work.

It’s a vision of a real relationship accessible to all.
A place where people feel fully seen for the ﬁrst time.

Patients and therapists.

Where do you need to be?
 The mental health landscape is crowded with the wrong things.

 Cash-pay therapy built on pleasing patients. Insurance platforms built
 on unsupported 1099 clinicians. Agencies that take half a session fee.
 A ﬁeld that treats insight as an afterthought and worksheets as salvation.
 Everyone is selling tools, tips and templated care.

 But none of that makes people feel understood. And none of it scales quality.

 The Inzinna vision is a quiet rebellion of it all. A practice built to challenge the
 category conventions.

 That means a team-based model with real training and support. Real
 understanding. All delivered through insurance — because access isn’t optional.

 The most trusted place for real therapy that normal people can afford and
 clinicians can sustain.

How
 Brand Identity and Name

As Inzinna prepares to leverage its positioning and scale the practice, the name
and logo must be considered.
Our conclusion:
 • Retain “Inzinna” as the dominant master brand.
 • Drop “Dr.” to avoid seeming like a private practice.
 • Soften/subordinate “Psychological Services” to anchor in the category.
 • Leverage a new tag line to differentiate.
 • Move to a clean, conﬁdent wordmark logo approach.
