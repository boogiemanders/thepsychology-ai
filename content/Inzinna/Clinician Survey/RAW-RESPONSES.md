## Open-Ended Responses (verbatim)

### Q: What features would make an AI clinical training tool WORTH USING regularly?

- **P01:** "ability to autosave notes on it's own"
- **P02:** "Insurance Checks"
- **P03:** "I'm not sure what would make an AI clinical training tool worth using in my current practice; I currently see it as something with more value as an administrative tool."
- **P04:** "Increased access to specialized training on modalities and assessment, administrative support (e.g., notes, scheduling, etc.), personalized client materials (e.g., letters, worksheets, meditations, etc.)"
- **P05:** "Outside of our assessment project, the ability to scaffold treatment planning on an ongoing basis prior to session based on occurrences in sessions, discussion of goals, and conceptualization of issues drawn from interpretations in session."
- **P07:** "If it could totally replace scheduling and communicating with patients outside of session."
- **P08:** "I find Ai very helpful. the note taking is very useful"

### Q: What are your concerns about using AI?

- **P01:** "it adds too much detail"
- **P02:** "Consistent Reliability"
- **P03:** "Accuracy, access to personal data, and inability to relate interpersonally"
- **P04:** "Client confidentiality and discomfort with use of AI. Diminishment of human connection and reduced therapeutic alliance."
- **P05:** "Any data being collected by outside entities being used to automate our work or compromise privacy. I am not certain I actually believe session transcripts or other data points are not being secretly maintained."
- **P07:** "Privacy and lack of human touch."
- **P08:** "I have no concerns."

### Q: What do you think an AI tool for clinical work would look like?

- **P01:** "should automatically save for indefinite amount of time"
- **P02:** "a trusted research companion, who knows how DIPs Operates"
- **P03:** "I imagine something that functions like an administrative assistant"
- **P04:** "Access to after-session notes and treatment plans based on session content. Generation of relevant questions and agenda for supervision. Increased access to specialized training materials based on session content. Generation of personalized materials for clients based on session content and treatment goals."
- **P05:** "Easily accessible assistant available prior/during/after sessions"
- **P07:** "What do you mean look like? Maybe a chat assistant native to the browser?"
- **P08:** "Note taking"

## Profile highlights

- **P07** — most enthusiastic, already uses AI note-taker, 5 min of doc time/week. Highest fit scores. Dealbreakers: none.
- **P08** — "I have no concerns." Eager adopter, newest to practice, wants assessments + supervision support.
- **P02** — pragmatic, admin-focused. #1 priority is VOB automation. Existing ZocDoc→SP extension directly addresses this.
- **P04** — most thoughtful; her #1 priority (specialized modality training) is the only priority not yet in the tool roadmap.
- **P03** — most skeptical (Likert mostly 1–4). Psychodynamic; sees AI as admin tool only. Multiple dealbreakers.
- **P05** — strong interest + strong privacy paranoia ("not certain transcripts aren't secretly maintained"). Assessment-focused priorities.
- **P01** — short answers; selective about privacy (2 dealbreakers). Wants autosave + training.
