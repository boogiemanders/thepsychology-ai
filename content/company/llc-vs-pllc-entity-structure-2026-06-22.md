# Entity Structure: LLC vs PLLC (2026-06-22)

Not legal or tax advice. Confirm with a NY CPA and a NY business attorney before filing. The deciding fact: Anders is NY-licensed (license #028257) with two very different income types.

## 1. Alma / clinical income -> PLLC (not a plain LLC)

- NY law requires a licensed professional (psychologist) who bills for professional services through an entity to use a PLLC or a PC. A regular LLC legally cannot deliver psychological services in NY.
- A NY PLLC needs NYSED Office of the Professions consent before filing, all owners must be licensed, and NY has a newspaper publication requirement (a few hundred dollars). A NY formation attorney handles this routinely.
- The real reason is malpractice/liability separation, not tax. Pair the PLLC with malpractice insurance.
- Check first: marketplaces like Alma often pay the individual clinician, not an entity. If Alma will not pay a PLLC, the PLLC will not cleanly capture that income. Ask Alma and the CPA before filing.

## 2. thePsychology.ai -> regular LLC

- It is an education software product, not the practice of psychology, so it does not need a PLLC. A normal LLC fits.
- Keep it separate from the clinical entity. Different liability (software/consumer/IP vs malpractice), and a PLLC generally cannot have non-licensed owners while a regular LLC can (matters if a cofounder or investor ever joins the app).

## Should you form anything now?

- The trigger is not a dollar line, it is "ongoing real revenue with liability exposure." Both income streams qualify.
- An LLC by itself is no tax change (pass-through, same as now). The tax win comes later: once net profit is meaningful (CPAs often float ~$40k+ of self-employment income as a rough start), elect S-corp taxation to cut self-employment tax. That is a numbers call with the CPA.

## Clean structure

Two entities: a PLLC for Alma/clinical, a regular LLC for thePsychology.ai. Separate bank accounts, never commingle. One CPA consult plus one attorney consult to set up.
