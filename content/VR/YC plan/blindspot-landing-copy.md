## PROBLEM

Current VR therapy is a one-way video.

You put on the headset. Content plays. Your nervous system has no say in what happens next.

The session does not know if you are anxious. It does not know if you are calm. It does not know if your eyes are dodging the thing you came to face.

If your therapist could see your body live, would they really stick to the same script?

---

## AGITATE

A single therapy session today:
- 50 minutes of guesswork
- 0 physiological signal
- Outcome measured by one self-report scale
- Decision to continue or change protocol made on vibes

EaseVRx, the only FDA-cleared VR therapeutic, is the closest thing the field has to proof. n=1,093, 12 months of durability data.

It still plays at you. The session does not adapt to your body.

---

## SOLUTION

Blind Spot is closed-loop VR therapy.

EEG. Heart rate variability. Eye gaze. The session reads all three live and adjusts in real time.

Breathe slowly, the world responds. Your gaze avoids the virtual speaking podium, the system notices. Your heart rate spikes during a tough interview question, you get a breathing cue and the session tracks how fast you recover.

Same evidence-based mechanism EaseVRx uses for breath-paced pain therapy. Plus a closed loop they never built.

## FAQ

**Is this an FDA-cleared medical device?**
Not yet. Phase 1 launches as a wellness device, same path EaseVRx took before its FDA clearance. We pursue FDA De Novo at Phase 2.

**Why not just use a meditation app?**
Meditation apps do not see what your nervous system is doing. They play the same audio whether you are calm or panicking. Closed-loop biofeedback responds.

**Why not just use VR therapy that already exists?**
Existing VR therapy is open-loop. The session content does not change based on your physiology. Ours does.

**Who is this for right now?**
High-functioning adults with social anxiety, performance anxiety, or stress that interferes with work. Researchers and clinicians interested in piloting.

**What hardware do I need?**
Meta Quest 3 plus a Muse S headband at Phase 1. Apple Watch HRV and Quest 3 built-in eye tracking on the roadmap.
