# VR + EEG + HRV for Flexor Tendon Hand Rehab (Zone I FDP)

Source: OpenEvidence research synthesis. Personal use case: Tamilyn ring finger DIP injury, Zone I FDP repair.

## Bottom Line

- Zone I FDP injury at DIP joint volar side. Ring finger = 75% of jersey finger avulsions.
- Repair strand count determines what rehab is safe. 2-strand = passive only, 26% rupture. 6-strand + palmar plate = true early active motion, ZERO ruptures.
- VR cuts pain (ocean nature scene d=1.60), kinesiophobia (StableHandVR RCT n=150: +63% adherence vs control), and adhesion risk via objective ROM tracking.
- EEG monitors pain state in real time (alpha up = mindfulness path, gamma up = immersion path). Beta/theta ratio rising = pain rising.
- HRV-B before exercise activates parasympathetic, reduces baseline pain. RMSSD drop during exercise = stress/pain spike, pause + breathe reset.
- Social presence in VR (companion or stranger) raises pain thresholds. Anders joining sessions adds measurable analgesia.
- Functional ring finger DIP target: 10-64 degrees.

## Surgery Snapshot

Zone I FDP = cut tendon at DIP joint volar side. If dorsal cut, that's mallet finger (extensor, splint only).

Ring finger uniquely vulnerable: weakest FDP insertion, extends furthest in grip. Sharp lac (vs avulsion) often has very short distal stump (<1cm).

| Repair | Strands | Rupture | Rehab Allowed | Outcome |
|--------|---------|---------|---------------|---------|
| Modified Kessler | 2 | 26% | Passive only (Kleinert) | Poor |
| Pennington-Kessler | 2 (core) | 11.4% reop | Limited early active | Moderate |
| Tsuge / pull-out | 4 | 2 ruptures (no palmar plate) | Early active | Good (87% good/excellent) |
| 6-strand + palmar plate | 6 | ZERO | True early active, even out-of-splint | Excellent |
| Bone anchor | 2 (anchored) | Zero (n=12) | Modified Belfast | Good (DIP ROM 57°) |

**Question for surgeon:** strand count, palmar plate incorporated, bone anchors used. This determines safe rehab timeline AND VR protocol intensity.

## Rehab Protocol (Evans, Zone I)

- **Weeks 0-3:** Dorsal blocking splint (wrist 30° flex, MCP 40° flex, PIP neutral). DIP splinted at 40-45° flex. Limited arc active flex (45-75°), ~3mm tendon excursion. **40-80 reps, 4-6 sessions/day.**
- **Weeks 3-6:** Progressive arcs. DIP block reduced. RMF orthosis: ring MCP held 20-30° more flex than neighbors (quadriga effect reduces force on repair).
- **Weeks 6-8:** Full active flex/ext. Gentle passive if stiff.
- **Weeks 8-12:** Progressive resistance.
- **Week 12+:** No restrictions.

RMF protocol across Zone I/II: ZERO ruptures, zero secondary surgery, zero PIP contractures. DIP active 0-75° for sharp lacs. Grip 62-108% of dominant.

**Predictors of bad outcome:** older age, smoking, skeletal injury, delay to surgery, 2-strand repair, immobilization.

## VR Adds Value at 4 Pain Points

### 1. Pain during exercises

Ocean nature VR: d=1.60 for pain tolerance. Driven by mood + enjoyment, not just distraction. Two pathways: gamma (immersion) + alpha (mindfulness). Both reduce P2 cortical pain response.

VR + HRV-B in TKA patients: more pain reduction than 2D video (p<0.01).

### 2. Kinesiophobia (the #1 barrier)

Telerehab causes higher kinesiophobia than face-to-face (ES=3.265). Top barrier: fear of damaging repair.

StableHandVR RCT (n=150): gamified VR with optical hand tracking → patients voluntarily exceeded prescribed volume by **63%**. Green-zone overlay confirms safe ROM in real time.

### 3. Adherence

40-80 reps × 4-6 daily for weeks. Non-adherence is THE modifiable risk factor.

VR turns it into a game. 1.5× more wrist ROM gain vs conventional PT (glove-VR study).

### 4. Adhesion detection

Tenolysis needed in 2-23%. Adhesions form in weeks 3-6.

VR auto ROM tracking flags plateau or regression early → catch before tenolysis is needed.

## EEG Adds Value

Real-time pain biomarkers from consumer-grade Muse 2/S:

- **Alpha up:** mindfulness path active. Hold the calm scene.
- **Gamma up:** immersion path active. Hold the engaging scene.
- **Beta/theta ratio rising:** pain rising. Switch scene OR reduce required ROM this set.
- **Baseline alpha decreasing over weeks:** central sensitization developing. Flag for intervention before chronic pain locks in.

EEG NFB meta-analysis: clinically meaningful short-term pain reduction. Reward Tamilyn's brain when she produces more alpha (visual feedback in VR scene).

## HRV Adds Value

HRV-pain link in 6/8 post-surgical studies. HRV-B rated **high quality** evidence for pain.

Mechanism: ~6 breaths/min + visual HRV feedback → vagus activation → parasympathetic up → descending pain inhibition.

**Daily VR session structure:**

1. **Pre (5 min):** VR breathing in nature scene at resonance freq. Virtual flower blooms as HRV rises. Activates parasympathetic before exercises.
2. **During (15-20 min):** Gamified hand exercise scene. Optical hand tracking. Green zone = safe ROM. Continuous HRV monitor. RMSSD drops sharply → pause + breathe reset → resume.
3. **Post (5 min):** Nature/mindfulness scene. Track HRV recovery speed. EEG-guided alpha reward.
4. **Anders joins 1-2x/week:** avatar in same scene. Social presence alone raises pain thresholds (RCT n=70).
5. **Weekly review:** ROM trend, HRV recovery, adherence. Plateau → flag therapist.

**Kinesiophobia detector:** sudden HRV drop near end-range DIP flex WITHOUT actual tissue damage = anticipatory fear, not nociception. System distinguishes and gives reassurance, doesn't reduce intensity.

## Social Support (Anders' Role)

Trauma study n=907: weak/no social support → significantly worse functional limitations, chronic pain, PTSD/depression/anxiety at 6-12 months. AAOS/METRC guideline: significant other relationships markedly affect outcomes.

VR companion RCT (n=70): social interaction in VR raises pain thresholds. Companion preferred over all conditions.

Anders should:

- Join in VR during sessions (avatar present)
- Do gamified exercises together
- Monitor ROM/HRV data, give informed encouragement

## Recovery Timeline (Optimal)

- **Week 6:** DIP active flex 30-45°. PIP recovering.
- **Week 12:** DIP active flex 45-60°. Grip 60%+ of contralateral. 94% return to work by here.
- **6 months:** DIP 48-57°. PIP 95-96°. Total active 142° (81% normal).
- **12 months:** Final. 56-94% excellent/good.

**Functional DIP target ring finger:** 10-64°. Ring needs MORE DIP range than index/long.

**Highest-value windows for VR:**

- Weeks 3-6: rupture vs adhesion balance. ROM monitoring + pain-gated progression.
- Weeks 6-12: stiffness window. Adherence and engagement matter most.
