# 16-Week, 5-Phase Integrated Chronic Pain Protocol: Session-by-Session

Detailed, session-by-session protocol synthesized from the best available evidence for each component. This is a proposed 16-week, 5-phase integrated protocol grounded in published treatment structures, with specific timing, patient-facing content, and measurement points.

---

## PHASE 0: BASELINE ASSESSMENT (Week 0 — 2 visits)

**What the patient experiences:**

- **Visit 1 (90 min, in-clinic):** Comprehensive intake with a pain physician. Medical history, review of imaging, and assessment of centralized vs. peripheral pain contributions (modeled on the PRT physician evaluation). Standardized questionnaires administered: BPI, PROMIS Pain Interference, PCS (Pain Catastrophizing Scale), TSK (Tampa Scale of Kinesiophobia), CPAQ (Chronic Pain Acceptance Questionnaire), PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5 (if trauma history). [1]
- **Visit 2 (60 min, in-clinic):** Resting-state EEG recording (5 min eyes-open, 5 min eyes-closed, 19-channel or minimum Cz/C3/C4/Fz/Pz) and 5-minute HRV recording (ECG or PPG) during paced breathing at 4.5-7 breaths/min to identify individual resonance frequency (RF). A short quantitative sensory testing battery may be included for deep phenotyping. [2][3]

**What is measured and why:**

- **EEG:** Theta (4-8 Hz), alpha (8-12 Hz), SMR (12-15 Hz), beta (15-30 Hz) power at central sites (C3, Cz, C4). Theta/alpha ratio and alpha peak frequency are extracted. Elevated theta and reduced alpha power have been consistently associated with chronic pain and may predict differential treatment response. [4][5][6]
- **HRV:** Time-domain (RMSSD, SDNN) and frequency-domain (LF, HF, LF/HF ratio) indices. The resonance frequency (~0.1 Hz, typically 4.5-6.5 breaths/min) is identified for each patient by testing breathing rates from 4 to 7 breaths/min in 0.5-breath increments and selecting the rate producing the highest HRV amplitude. [2][3]
- **Phenotype assignment:** Based on the composite profile, patients are triaged to one of three psychotherapy tracks (see Phase 2).

## PHASE 1: VR PAIN NEUROSCIENCE EDUCATION (Weeks 1-2 — 4 sessions)

**What the patient experiences:**

- **Sessions 1-4 (30-45 min each, 2×/week, in-clinic or home VR headset):** Immersive VR modules delivering pain neuroscience education. Content includes animated 3D visualizations of how the brain generates and amplifies pain signals, the difference between acute nociception and chronic nociplastic pain, and interactive demonstrations of how attention, fear, and beliefs modulate pain. This is modeled on the VRNT protocol (Čeko et al.), which produced effect sizes of g = 0.63-0.84. [7]
- Sessions include interactive elements: patients explore a virtual nervous system, manipulate a "threat dial" to see how fear amplifies pain signals, and watch testimonials from recovered patients (a key PRT element). [8]
- Session 2 introduces the concept of **somatic tracking** — attending to pain sensations with curiosity rather than fear — practiced first in VR (safe environment) before real-world application. [1]
- Session 4 concludes with a personalized summary: the physician's assessment of centralized pain contributions is presented in VR alongside the patient's own imaging, reframing findings (e.g., "your MRI shows age-normal changes, not damage").

**What is measured:**

- Pre/post each session: NRS pain intensity (0-10), pain-related fear (single item), and self-efficacy (single item)
- Session 4: Repeat PCS and TSK to capture early shifts in catastrophizing and kinesiophobia (expected to begin declining, as these mediated VRNT effects). [7]

## PHASE 2: TARGETED PSYCHOTHERAPY CORE (Weeks 3-8 — 8 sessions, 2×/week)

Patients are assigned to one of three tracks based on Phase 0 phenotyping.

### Track A — Pain Reprocessing Therapy (PRT)

**Best for:** Patients with high pain catastrophizing, strong fear-avoidance beliefs, and pain attributed primarily to tissue damage. EEG profile: elevated theta, reduced alpha.

Session-by-session content (modeled directly on the Ashar et al. protocol): [1]

- **Sessions 1-2:** Personalized evidence for centralized pain; guided reappraisal of pain sensations while seated (somatic tracking with safety reappraisal — "this sensation is not dangerous")
- **Sessions 3-4:** Guided reappraisal during feared postures and movements (graded exposure with cognitive reattribution)
- **Sessions 5-6:** Addressing psychosocial threats — identifying emotional triggers that amplify pain; linking emotional states to pain flares
- **Sessions 7-8:** Increasing positive emotions and self-compassion; consolidation and relapse prevention
- Each session is 50-60 min, delivered by a trained therapist (telehealth or in-person)

### Track B — Emotional Awareness and Expression Therapy (EAET)

**Best for:** Patients with significant trauma history (elevated PCL-5), emotional suppression, or alexithymia. HRV profile: markedly reduced vagal tone.

- **Sessions 1-2:** Psychoeducation on the role of emotions in pain; identifying avoided or suppressed emotions
- **Sessions 3-6:** Experiential processing of unresolved emotional conflicts (anger, grief, guilt) linked to pain onset or maintenance; written emotional disclosure exercises
- **Sessions 7-8:** Assertiveness training, adaptive emotional expression, and consolidation [9]

### Track C — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

**Best for:** Patients with high psychological inflexibility, behavioral disengagement, and functional disability disproportionate to pain severity.

- **Sessions 1-2:** Creative hopelessness (examining the costs of control-based strategies); values clarification
- **Sessions 3-4:** Mindfulness and defusion exercises (observing pain thoughts without fusion); acceptance of pain sensations
- **Sessions 5-6:** Committed action toward identified values despite pain; graded behavioral activation
- **Sessions 7-8:** Self-as-context exercises; relapse prevention and maintenance planning
- Typical ACT protocols use 8 weekly sessions, but the twice-weekly schedule is adopted here based on evidence that treatment effects emerge by session 6 regardless of modality. [10][11][12]

**What is measured:**

- Weekly: NRS pain, BPI interference, PCS, CPAQ (for ACT track), pain attribution scale (for PRT track)
- **Week 5 (mid-treatment decision point):** If <20% improvement in BPI interference, consider augmenting with biofeedback (Phase 3 begins concurrently). If ≥50% improvement, biofeedback may be optional. This adaptive design mirrors the SMART framework used in the OPTIMIZE trial. [13][14]

## PHASE 3: VR-AUGMENTED BIOFEEDBACK (Weeks 5-12 — 16 sessions)

This phase runs concurrently with the second half of Phase 2 and continues 4 weeks beyond it. Sessions are 2×/week for 8 weeks.

### Component A: VR-HRV Biofeedback (alternating sessions)

**What the patient experiences:**

- Patient wears a VR headset with integrated PPG/ECG sensor. In the virtual environment, they see a calming landscape (e.g., beach, forest) where visual elements respond in real time to their HRV: as coherence increases, the environment becomes more vivid, flowers bloom, waves synchronize. [15][16]
- Patient breathes at their individually determined resonance frequency (identified in Phase 0), guided by a visual pacer in VR
- Each session: 5 min baseline recording → 20 min resonance frequency breathing with VR feedback → 5 min post-training baseline
- Evidence indicates 4 sessions minimum are needed to produce measurable HRV increases and breathing rate decreases, with effects consolidating over weeks 3-4 of training. [17]

**What is measured:**

- Real-time during each session: RMSSD, HF-HRV, LF power, coherence ratio, breathing rate
- Pre/post each session: NRS pain, anxiety (single item)
- The target is progressive increase in resting RMSSD and HF-HRV across sessions, reflecting restored vagal tone. [18][19]

### Component B: EEG Neurofeedback (alternating sessions)

**What the patient experiences:**

- Patient sits comfortably with EEG electrodes at C4 (or Cz, contralateral to pain side for lateralized pain). The most common evidence-based protocol reinforces alpha (8-12 Hz) or SMR (12-15 Hz) while suppressing theta (4-8 Hz) and high-beta (20-30 Hz). [20][21]
- Feedback is delivered via a VR environment or screen-based game: when the target EEG ratio improves, the patient receives visual/auditory rewards (e.g., a spaceship accelerates, a puzzle piece appears)
- Each session: 5 min eyes-open baseline EEG → 20 min neurofeedback training (4 × 5-min blocks with 1-min rest) → 5 min post-training baseline
- For neuropathic pain specifically, a β1/β2 ratio protocol at central sites contralateral to pain has shown specific analgesic effects in 12 sessions over 4 weeks. [22]
- For patients with prominent anxiety/depression, an α/θ ratio protocol may be preferred, as this specifically reduced anxiety and depression in the Mussigmann et al. trial. [22]

**What is measured:**

- Real-time: Target frequency band power, ratio metrics, percentage of time above threshold
- Pre/post each session: NRS pain, brief mood scale
- Week 8 and Week 12: Full resting-state EEG to assess whether training-induced changes persist at rest (the key question of "transfer"). [22][23]

## PHASE 4: INTEGRATION AND GRADED EXPOSURE (Weeks 9-12 — 4 sessions)

**What the patient experiences:**

- **Sessions 1-2 (in-clinic, 60 min):** Combined session integrating all modalities. Patient begins with 10 min HRV biofeedback breathing to achieve autonomic calm → transitions to VR graded exposure (performing feared movements in VR while practicing somatic tracking from PRT or acceptance from ACT) → therapist guides cognitive reappraisal in real time
- **Sessions 3-4 (in-clinic or home, 60 min):** Real-world graded exposure with wearable HRV monitoring. Patient performs previously feared physical activities (bending, lifting, exercise) while wearing a wearable HRV monitor that provides real-time feedback on their autonomic state. Therapist (in-person or via telehealth) coaches somatic tracking and reappraisal during the activity. This bridges VR-based learning to real-world generalization. [24][25]

**What is measured:**

- Continuous HRV during real-world exposure (wearable)
- Pre/post exposure: NRS pain, fear of movement (single item), self-efficacy
- **Week 12 comprehensive reassessment:** Full battery (BPI, PCS, TSK, CPAQ, PHQ-9, GAD-7, PROMIS), resting EEG, resting HRV. This is the primary endpoint.

---

## PHASE 5: MAINTENANCE AND SELF-MANAGEMENT (Weeks 13-16 and beyond)

**What the patient experiences:**

- **Home VR program:** Patient retains VR headset for daily 15-min self-guided sessions. Content includes relaxation environments, guided somatic tracking exercises, pain neuroscience refreshers, and graded movement games. The EaseVRx program demonstrated sustained benefits over 8 weeks of daily home use with effect sizes of 0.40-0.49 vs. sham. The Vx Therapy program showed increasing duration of pain relief from 2.5 hours (month 1) to 4.5 hours (month 3-4) with daily use. [26][27]
- **Wearable HRV practice:** Daily 10-min resonance frequency breathing using a consumer wearable (e.g., chest strap + app), with weekly data uploaded for clinician review
- **Booster sessions:** 2 therapist check-ins (weeks 14 and 16, 30 min each, telehealth) reviewing progress, troubleshooting, and reinforcing key skills
- **Optional home EEG neurofeedback:** For patients who responded to in-clinic neurofeedback, a home-based device (e.g., Axon system) can deliver 32-48 additional sessions over 8 weeks. The home-based proof-of-concept showed 69% of patients who upregulated alpha reported improved pain. [28]

**What is measured:**

- Weekly via app: NRS pain, BPI interference (2-item), single-item mood and sleep quality
- **Week 16 (secondary endpoint):** Full reassessment battery
- **Week 28 and Week 52 (follow-up):** Full battery to assess durability (modeled on OPTIMIZE 26- and 52-week follow-ups). [13]

## Key Design Rationale

The twice-weekly session frequency during the psychotherapy core is based on the PRT trial structure (8 sessions over 4 weeks) and evidence that treatment effects typically emerge by session 6. [1][10] The adaptive decision point at Week 5 mirrors the SMART design used in the OPTIMIZE trial, where non-responders to first-line treatment were re-randomized — here, non-responders receive biofeedback augmentation rather than treatment switching. [13][14] The 12-session neurofeedback dose is based on the Mussigmann et al. neuropathic pain trial (12 sessions over 4 weeks showing significant analgesia), while the 32-session home-based extension follows the Birch et al. and Rice et al. protocols. [22][28][23] The HRV biofeedback structure follows the Lehrer resonance frequency protocol widely used across 143 studies, with the minimum effective dose of 4 sessions established by Lin et al. [3][17]

It bears emphasis that this integrated protocol remains untested as a whole. Each component draws from the strongest available evidence for that modality, but the additive or synergistic effects of combining them are hypothetical. The adaptive SMART framework provides a principled structure for testing this in a clinical trial.
