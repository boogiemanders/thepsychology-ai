# The Heart-Wall

Source: Ch 8 "The Walls Around Our Hearts". The flagship concept of the Emotion Code.

## What it is

- When the heart takes repeated emotional damage, the subconscious builds a **protective shell** around it.
- The building material: trapped emotions, stacked layer by layer.
- The wall is described as energy on a "different plane of reality" but tangible to the subconscious.
- Subjects often see their wall as a specific material in imagery (Jean's was a hardwood floor; others describe brick, steel, glass, etc).

## Why it forms

- Single overwhelming emotional events (a sudden loss, abuse, trauma).
- Sustained low-grade emotional environment (chronic conflict, emotional neglect, abusive marriage).
- Often the first layers go down in **early childhood** — under age 5 is common.
- "Like hiding in a bunker during a bombing." Short-term: lifesaving. Long-term: cuts you off from joy too.

## Prevalence

- Nelson's clinical observation: **~93% of people have one**.
- Children who haven't had prolonged hardship are the main exceptions.

## Symptoms a Heart-Wall is in play

- Chronic feeling of being on the outside, disconnected, "looking in".
- Numbness to positive emotion. Easy access to negative emotion.
- Can't fall in love / can't stay in love / phobic of relationships.
- Mysterious neck, shoulder, or chest tension that no doctor can explain.
- Persistent depression that doesn't respond to traditional treatment.
- Initial protocol question ("do you have a trapped emotion that can be released now?") returns NO when you know there should be one — the wall is blocking access.

## Identification

**Sequence:**
1. Run normal baseline.
2. Ask: **"Do I (you) have a Heart-Wall?"** → yes/no via muscle test.
3. If yes: **"Can we release a trapped emotion from the Heart-Wall right now?"**
4. If yes: enter the standard funnel from [[03-protocol-release-session]] to identify which emotion.

The wall must be addressed **emotion by emotion**, layer by layer. Same as any other release, except:
- Phrasing always specifies "from my Heart-Wall".
- Release uses **10 sweeps** down the Governing Meridian, not 3.

## Release per emotion

Same as standard protocol, with these modifications:

| Difference | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sweeps | **10** down the Governing Meridian, not 3 |
| Intention | "Release this emotion from my Heart-Wall" |
| Pacing | Body may allow several per session, or only one. Honor what the subconscious says |
| Material imagery | Often surfaces during identification — note it, ignore if it doesn't |

## Average count

- Most Heart-Walls = **7-10 trapped emotions** stacked.
- Joanne's example in Ch 8: 9 emotions, all related to abusive husband, cleared in ~30 minutes total.
- Some take weeks/months across multiple sessions.

## What happens after clearance

- Subject often experiences: a sense of belonging, reconnection, willingness to feel.
- ~30% of cases show dramatic immediate change. The other 70% shift gradually.
- The book reports relationship changes (couples reconnecting, first dating in years), career changes, creative breakthroughs.
- Physical symptoms tied to the wall (chronic pain in upper body) often resolve.
- **Warning**: removing the wall exposes the person to the actual emotional environment. If the environment is toxic (e.g. ongoing abuse), they may now feel the full weight of what the wall was suppressing.

## Inherited Heart-Wall

A Heart-Wall can also be inherited — formed by an ancestor and energetically passed down. Release uses 10 sweeps with intention to release from all descendants.
