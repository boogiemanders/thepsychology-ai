# Companion Interview — "The Heart of the Emotion Code"

Source: *The Heart of the Emotion Code* by Ric Thompson + Dr Bradley Nelson (Healthy Wealthy nWise Press, 2011). 27-page interview / introduction. Most of the protocol is already in the main book; this doc captures **what's unique** to the companion.

## What the companion adds

1. **Origin story of the Heart-Wall discovery** told first-person from Nelson, more vivid than in the main book.
2. **The "energy" framing**, leaned into harder than in the main book — atoms as 99.99...% empty space, body as organized energy field.
3. **Heart as second brain** — cites the heart's electromagnetic field measuring up to 12 feet in diameter (MagnetoCardiogram). Cites organ-transplant anecdotes (Snickers craving, shopping urge, etc) as evidence the heart carries personality / memory.
4. **Two case-study patient stories** told in detail:
   - Woman with crushing chest pain, numb arm/face — looked like a heart attack. Trapped grief from her husband's affair 3 years prior. Released; symptoms gone in 5 seconds.
   - 72-year-old woman with trapped sadness traced to 1963 (JFK assassination day). The energy ball had been lodged in her left breast area. She had had that breast removed for malignancy 4 years before the session.
5. **The Heart-Wall origin** — Nelson's wife Jean had a waking vision of a hardwood floor with her heart underneath it. Took 2 weeks to clear. He thought she was the only one. Later: ~93% prevalence.
6. **The Steve Jobs framing** — "follow your heart" interpreted literally. Suggests Jobs likely didn't have a Heart-Wall, which is why he could create from a clear channel.
7. **The 11-year-old kid using the book** — a Los Angeles seminar story. Kid read the book, ran the protocol on a friend with a water phobia. Friend went swimming the next day. Mentioned to make the point: protocol is simple enough for a child to run.

## Nelson's bio (added detail)

- Holistic chiropractic physician + craniopath.
- Graduated Life Chiropractic College West (San Lorenzo, CA) 1988.
- Private clinical practice until 2004.
- Wife Liz / Jean (different sources reference both names), 7 children.
- Based in Southern Utah.
- Teaches certification program: practitioners worldwide.

## Tone differences

The companion is **more devotional / spiritual** than the main book:
- Heavier emphasis on prayer, divine assistance, "God above who loves every one of us."
- Frames Emotion Code as a religious gift.
- The main book has some of this but tries to also speak to a secular audience.

For a clinical product, the companion's framing is **too religious to translate directly**. The main book's mechanics + the companion's case stories are the useful inputs.

## Heart-as-creative-source claim

Multiple times: "every person has within their heart a perfect creation that they can manifest into the world." Heart-Wall blocks this. Removing the wall releases creative energy.

This is the angle to lean into for a wellness/coaching market positioning. Not provable, but emotionally resonant and consistent with somatic-psychology trends.

## What's NOT in the companion that's in the main book

- The full Emotion Code Chart (only referenced, not reproduced).
- Detailed protocol steps for inherited emotions and lineage testing.
- Surrogate / proxy detail (mentioned in passing).
- The 6 testing methods (only sway test described).
- Animal-testing details (only short anecdote).
