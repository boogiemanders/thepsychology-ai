# The Emotion Code Chart

Source: *The Emotion Code* (Nelson, 2019), chart shown in Ch 2 and Ch 6. Glossary entries from "Glossary of Emotions" appendix.

## Structure

- **2 columns** (A, B)
- **6 rows**, each tied to a pair of organs / meridian
- **5 emotions per cell** → 60 emotions total
- Each emotion is a distinct **vibration**. Words are approximations; the subconscious will pick the closest match when an exact one isn't listed.

## Full chart

| Row | Organs | Column A | Column B |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Heart / Small Intestine | Abandonment, Betrayal, Forlorn, Lost, Love Unreceived | Effort Unreceived, Heartache, Insecurity, Overjoy, Vulnerability |
| **2** | Spleen / Stomach | Anxiety, Despair, Disgust, Nervousness, Worry | Failure, Helplessness, Hopelessness, Lack of Control, Low Self-Esteem |
| **3** | Lung / Colon | Crying, Discouragement, Rejection, Sadness, Sorrow | Confusion, Defensiveness, Grief, Self-Abuse, Stubbornness |
| **4** | Liver / Gallbladder | Anger, Bitterness, Guilt, Hatred, Resentment | Depression, Frustration, Indecisiveness, Panic, Taken for Granted |
| **5** | Kidneys / Bladder | Blaming, Dread, Fear, Horror, Peeved | Conflict, Creative Insecurity, Terror, Unsupported, Wishy Washy |
| **6** | Glands / Sexual Organs | Humiliation, Jealousy, Longing, Lust, Overwhelm | Pride, Shame, Shock, Unworthy, Worthless |

## Organ-to-emotion mapping (book-asserted)

Nelson says each organ "produces" the emotions in its row. From ancient Chinese medicine:
- Anger → Liver / Gallbladder (Row 4)
- Grief → Lung / Colon (Row 3)
- Fear → Kidneys / Bladder (Row 5)
- Worry/anxiety → Spleen / Stomach (Row 2)
- Betrayal/heartache → Heart / Small Intestine (Row 1)

## Glossary (book definitions, condensed)

### Row 1 — Heart / Small Intestine
- **Abandonment**: left, deserted (physical), or emotionally withdrawn from
- **Betrayal**: trust broken; deserted or hurt by a trusted person
- **Forlorn**: miserable, forsaken; lonely hopelessness
- **Lost**: no direction; childhood "physically lost" or adult emotional drift
- **Love Unreceived**: one's love rejected or unwanted; love that has nowhere to land
- **Effort Unreceived**: work/achievement unrecognized or unappreciated
- **Heartache**: anguish, pain in the chest; result of relational difficulty
- **Insecurity**: lack of confidence, self-consciousness, shyness
- **Overjoy**: joy too intense for the body to absorb — a shock-positive
- **Vulnerability**: feeling susceptible to harm, unsafe, unstable

### Row 2 — Spleen / Stomach
- **Anxiety**: generalized uneasy foreboding, fear without a known source
- **Despair**: complete loss of hope; miserable, beyond comfort
- **Disgust**: disapproval, loathing, strong aversion
- **Nervousness**: jumpy, on edge, apprehensive
- **Worry**: dwelling on troubles; persistent low-grade fear
- **Failure**: fell short of success in something attempted
- **Helplessness**: powerlessness; unable to help oneself
- **Hopelessness**: no expectation of good outcome
- **Lack of Control**: lacking self-control, or losing control over circumstances
- **Low Self-Esteem**: low appraisal of own worth; focus on flaws

### Row 3 — Lung / Colon
- **Crying**: expression of grief; or the suppression of crying when one needs to
- **Discouragement**: lack of courage/hope; disheartened
- **Rejection**: denied, refused, discarded, unwanted
- **Sadness**: unhappy, sorrowful, mournful
- **Sorrow**: regret; distress from loss or disappointment
- **Confusion**: disoriented, foggy, perplexed
- **Defensiveness**: resisting attack; on guard against threats real or imagined
- **Grief**: suffering due to loss, death, bereavement, unmet expectations
- **Self-Abuse**: emotional (negative self-talk) or physical (addictions, neglect of body)
- **Stubbornness**: obstinate, unbending, headstrong, unforgiving

### Row 4 — Liver / Gallbladder
- **Anger**: strong displeasure/hostility from real or perceived wrong
- **Bitterness**: harsh, disagreeable, resentful cynicism
- **Guilt**: feeling of having done wrong; or taking responsibility for another's harm
- **Hatred**: loathing, contempt; often "hurt love" turned cold
- **Resentment**: indignation at perceived unfair treatment; unwillingness to forgive
- **Depression**: anger turned inward; inability to feel joy; low-energy negative state
- **Frustration**: exasperated, blocked from progress
- **Indecisiveness**: wavering; distrust of own ability to choose
- **Panic**: sudden overwhelming fear; trembling, hyperventilation, impending doom
- **Taken for Granted**: treated with careless indifference; unthanked

### Row 5 — Kidneys / Bladder
- **Blaming**: being blamed, blaming others, or blaming self
- **Dread**: fear of something about to happen; apprehension about the future
- **Fear**: distress aroused by impending real or imagined danger
- **Horror**: alarm/outrage from something violent or shocking
- **Peeved**: irritated, annoyed, ticked off
- **Conflict**: internal struggle, or external fight/disagreement
- **Creative Insecurity**: unsafe/untrusting of self around creating anything (relationships, work, art)
- **Terror**: intense, sharp, overwhelming fear
- **Unsupported**: feeling unhelped, undefended, alone with the burden
- **Wishy Washy**: weak-willed, undecided, lacking conviction

### Row 6 — Glands / Sexual Organs
- **Humiliation**: painful loss of pride, dignity, self-respect; mortification
- **Jealousy**: envy of another's advantages; suspicion of rivalry
- **Longing**: missing, yearning, pining; wanting what you don't have
- **Lust**: intense sexual or grasping desire; covetousness; can be absorbed from an abuser
- **Overwhelm**: overpowered in mind/emotion; extreme stress
- **Pride**: overly high self-regard; vanity; nonteachable; "having to be right"
- **Shame**: feeling defective, dishonorable; the lowest-vibration emotion in the system
- **Shock**: sudden violent disturbance to the emotional system; feeling traumatized or stunned
- **Unworthy**: not good enough; undeserving
- **Worthless**: of no importance, no value, no purpose
