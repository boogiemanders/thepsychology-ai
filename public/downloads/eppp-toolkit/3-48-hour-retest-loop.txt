THE 48-HOUR RETEST LOOP
A system for turning missed questions into mastered content.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

HOW IT WORKS

Most people review what they got wrong, feel like they understand it,
and move on. Then they miss the same type of question two weeks later.

This loop forces actual retrieval — not recognition — by retesting
before the memory fades but after enough time has passed to require
real recall.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE LOOP

STEP 1: TAKE A PRACTICE SET
  - 20-30 questions from one domain
  - Time yourself (1.2 min per question)
  - No notes, no peeking

STEP 2: SCORE IT
  - Write down your raw score: ___/___
  - Do not rationalize. Do not explain away.

STEP 3: LOG EVERY MISS
  - Open your Mistake Log
  - For each wrong answer, categorize:
      Content Gap — You didn't know the material
      Misread — You knew it but read the question wrong
      Trap — You knew it but picked the distractor
  - Write what you thought AND what was correct

STEP 4: STUDY ONLY THE MISSES
  - Content Gaps: Go learn that specific concept (not the whole chapter)
  - Misreads: Reread the question — what word did you skip?
  - Traps: What made the wrong answer attractive?
  - Time limit: 30-45 minutes max

STEP 5: WAIT 48 HOURS
  - Do not retest tomorrow. Do not retest today.
  - 48 hours is the minimum spacing for durable retrieval.
  - Study other domains in the meantime.

STEP 6: RETEST
  - Take the SAME missed questions + 5 new ones from the same domain
  - Score it again

STEP 7: EVALUATE
  - 85% or higher → This domain loop is complete. Move on.
  - Below 85% → Back to Step 3. Re-log, re-study, wait 48 hours.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RULES

- Never skip Step 3. The log is where the learning happens.
- Never retest before 48 hours. Sooner feels productive but builds
  false confidence.
- Never study more than 45 minutes per loop. You're fixing gaps,
  not re-learning the domain.
- Track your loops: after 3 cycles, most domains stabilize above 85%.
