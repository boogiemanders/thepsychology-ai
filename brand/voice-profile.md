# thepsychology.ai Voice Profile

## Voice Summary
Sounds like a psychologist who's been through the worst of EPPP prep talking to someone who's in the middle of it right now. Vulnerable enough to say "I scored 19%" but sharp enough to follow it with a system that worked. Clinical precision meets real-talk honesty. Never sells — just shares what actually happened, with the numbers to back it up.

## Core Personality Traits
- **Vulnerable authority:** Leads with failure ("19%") before showing the win ("588 in 30 days"). Earns trust by admitting the struggle first, then proving the method works.
- **Clinical precision:** Uses data, frameworks, and evidence — because the audience is trained to think this way. References learning science they already know (testing effect, spaced repetition) to build immediate credibility.
- **Anti-BS directness:** "Not a pep talk. Not a sales pitch. A plan." Refuses to sugarcoat. Calls out what's broken in the prep industry without being preachy.
- **Empathetic peer:** Knows what the shame spiral feels like after a bad score. Doesn't talk down. Talks across — "I've felt it. Here's what I did."

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casual-professional | Contractions, direct address, but substantive. Not sloppy. |
| Serious ↔ Playful | Serious with dry wit | The topic is high-stakes. Occasional wry observations, never jokey. |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | Bold | "Not a typo. Nineteen percent." Makes strong claims backed by specifics. |
| Simple ↔ Sophisticated | Simple words, sophisticated ideas | References clinical concepts in plain language. Never jargon-heavy. |
| Warm ↔ Direct | Direct with warmth underneath | Doesn't coddle, but clearly cares. "I know those questions because I've sat with them myself." |

## Vocabulary

**Words/phrases to USE:**
- "Actually" — as in "what actually works" / "what actually moved the needle"
- "The truth is" / "Here's the reality" — transition into honest assessments
- "Not because I'm special" — vulnerability marker
- Specific numbers always — 19%, 588, 30 days, $849, $687.50, 225 questions
- "That's a different skill than..." — reframing device
- "Let's get into it" — section transition
- Clinical analogies — "like going to a doctor who prescribes treatment before running tests"
- "I get it. I was there." — empathy markers

**Words/phrases to AVOID:**
- "Comprehensive" / "robust" / "cutting-edge" / "game-changer" — corporate AI-speak
- "Unlock your potential" / "supercharge" / "level up" — guru energy
- "We believe" / "Our mission" — corporate distance
- "Delve" / "dive into" / "navigate" — AI tells
- "Passionate about" — empty
- "Just" (minimizing) — "just study harder" trivializes real struggle
- "Revolutionary" / "disruptive" — startup-speak the audience doesn't trust
- "AI-powered" as a lead benefit — they don't care about AI, they care about passing

**Jargon level:** Moderate — uses clinical and psychometric terms the audience already knows (testing effect, spaced repetition, active recall, domain weights) but always in service of a practical point, never to show off.

**Profanity:** Never. The audience is clinical professionals.

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Alternates short punchy ("Not a typo.") with medium explanatory (10-20 words). Rarely goes long. Uses fragments for impact.

**Paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences. Generous white space. Never walls of text.

**Openings:** Leads with a specific result or personal admission, never a generic statement. "I scored 19% on my first EPPP practice diagnostic exam." Not "Preparing for the EPPP can be challenging."

**Formatting:** Bold for key concepts. Tables for data. Numbered steps for processes. Minimal decoration. No emojis.

## POV & Address

**First person:** I (founder's perspective — personal, not corporate)
**Reader address:** You, direct and personal
**Relationship stance:** Peer who's slightly ahead. "I was where you are. Here's what I found." Not a guru. Not a coach. Someone who went through it and built the thing they wished existed.

## Platform Adaptations

| Platform | Tone Shift | Structure | Length |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Email | Warmer, more personal. "Hey" opener OK. | Short paragraphs, single CTA, personal story | 150-300 words |
| LinkedIn | Expertise-forward, still personal. Lead with insight. | Line breaks, hook first line, credential signal | 100-200 words |
| Twitter/X | Punchiest. One insight or stat per tweet. | Fragment sentences, specific numbers | Under 280 chars |
| Blog/SEO | Most thorough. Same voice, more depth. Teacher mode. | Headers, numbered steps, tables, examples | 1500-3000 words |
| Landing Page | Most urgent. Pain-first. Proof-heavy. Still personal. | Short sentences, pain quantification, clear CTA | Varies by section |

## Example Phrases

**On-brand (sounds like us):**
- "I scored 19% on my first diagnostic. Not a typo. Nineteen percent."
- "Most EPPP prep programs tell you to study for 6 to 9 months. I passed in 30."
- "Not a pep talk. Not a sales pitch. A plan."
- "You wouldn't accept that from a clinician. Don't accept it from yourself."
- "The people who fail are often the ones who assumed their doctoral education was enough."

**Off-brand (doesn't sound like us):**
- "Our comprehensive AI-powered platform helps you unlock your full EPPP potential." — Corporate, buzzwordy, no personality
- "Thousands of satisfied customers trust our proven methodology!" — Inflated, vague, salesy
- "Ready to crush the EPPP? Let's go!" — Bro energy, doesn't match the gravity of the situation
- "We leverage cutting-edge artificial intelligence to optimize your study experience." — Every word is wrong

## Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Lead with specific numbers and personal experience
- Acknowledge the emotional weight of EPPP prep
- Use clinical analogies the audience already understands
- Show the math — opportunity scores, cost comparisons, time calculations
- Write like you're talking to a smart colleague, not a customer

**DON'T:**
- Lead with features or technology ("AI-powered!")
- Promise easy results — this audience has been burned by promises
- Minimize the difficulty ("just study harder")
- Use startup/SaaS marketing language
- Hide behind "we" — the founder's personal story is the strongest asset
- Use emojis or exclamation marks
