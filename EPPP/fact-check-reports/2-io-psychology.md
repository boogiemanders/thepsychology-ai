### Finding 1 - JUDGMENT (confirmed accurate)
**Claim (Job Satisfaction; ~line 28, 254):** "about 30% of the variation in job satisfaction can be attributed to genetic factors" (identical-twins-reared-apart study).
**OE verdict:** Correct. Arvey et al. (1989) twin study estimated ~30% genetic variance in job satisfaction; widely replicated. OE notes this is indirect (via temperament/affectivity), not genes "for" satisfaction.
*Cite: Arvey et al., 1989 (classic reference per OE).*
**Suggested fix (lesson-voice):** None needed. Optional clarity add: "Genes don't directly set job satisfaction. They shape your personality and mood, which then color how you feel about work."

**Claim (~line 52, 211, 256):** satisfaction-performance correlation ".17 to .30."
**OE verdict:** Accurate range. Iaffaldano & Muchinsky (1985) ~.17; Judge et al. (2001) corrected ~.30. Range captures the literature's evolution.
*Cite: Judge et al., 2001; Iaffaldano & Muchinsky, 1985.*
**Suggested fix (lesson-voice):** None needed.

### Finding 3 - JUDGMENT (confirmed accurate)
**Claim (Type A; ~line 204, 223, 263):** hostility is the Type A component most strongly tied to cardiovascular risk.
**OE verdict:** Correct and current. AHA statements call hostility the "toxic" component; Chida & Steptoe (2009) meta-analysis links anger/hostility to ~19% higher incident CHD risk.
*Cite: Chida & Steptoe, J Am Coll Cardiol 2009; AHA scientific statements (Levine 2021, Fleg 2013).*
**Suggested fix (lesson-voice):** None needed.

## 5-hiring-methods.md
URL: /resources/topics/3-5-6-organizational-psychology/employee-selection-techniques

Verified against standard references (high confidence, not separately OE-queried): structured vs unstructured, behavioral vs situational interviews; GMA tests and their adverse-impact tradeoff; Big Five with conscientiousness as best personality predictor; overt vs personality-based integrity tests; work samples, trainability tests, realistic job previews; assessment centers, in-basket exercise, leaderless group discussion; biodata/BIB and face validity; compensatory (clinical prediction, multiple regression; statistical beats clinical) vs noncompensatory (multiple cutoff, multiple hurdles). OE returned no usable body for these non-clinical I-O queries; verdicts below rest on the Schmidt & Hunter (1998) and Sackett et al. (2022) meta-analytic literature.

### Finding 1 - FACTUAL-WRONG
**Claim (Interviews; ~line 32, 144, 190):** "Both structured and unstructured interviews have the same average validity coefficient of .58."
**OE verdict:** Incorrect under either major meta-analysis. The ~.51-.58 validity belongs to STRUCTURED interviews specifically. Unstructured interviews are meaningfully lower (~.38 in Schmidt & Hunter 1998; ~.19 operational in Sackett et al. 2022). They are not equal, and the lesson's own "Misconception 1" repeats this error.
*Cite: Schmidt & Hunter, 1998 (Psychol Bulletin); Sackett, Zhang, Berry & Lievens, 2022 (J Applied Psychology).*
**Suggested fix (lesson-voice):** "Structured interviews predict job performance much better than unstructured ones. A well-built structured interview can reach a validity around .51 to .58, while unstructured interviews fall well below that. So the structure really does matter." (And fix Misconception 1 to say structured interviews are stronger, not equal.)

### Finding 2 - JUDGMENT
**Claim (~line 32, 44, 160, 188):** GMA tests are "the single most valid predictor" and interviews are "the second-best predictor (after general mental ability tests)."
**OE verdict:** This is the classic Schmidt & Hunter (1998) hierarchy and is still the EPPP-tested answer, so it is not flagged as an error. Note: the 2022 Sackett et al. reanalysis revised GMA operational validity downward (~.31) and placed structured interviews and other methods at or above GMA. Diverges from current literature but matches the exam-accepted ranking.
*Cite: Sackett et al., 2022.*
**Suggested fix (lesson-voice):** Optional caveat: "Older research ranked general mental ability tests as the number-one predictor. Newer 2022 research lowered that ranking and put a strong structured interview at the top. For the exam, the older ranking is usually the expected answer."

### Finding 3 - JUDGMENT
**Claim (~line 40, 147, 192):** situational interview questions predict performance better than behavioral questions when both target the same requirements.
**OE verdict:** Mixed in the literature (some studies favor situational for lower-complexity jobs, behavioral for higher-complexity/senior roles). Defensible as the EPPP-accepted finding; flagged JUDGMENT, not an error.
**Suggested fix (lesson-voice):** None required for the exam. Could soften to "research has often found situational questions predict at least as well as behavioral ones."

**Excerpt sync:** grep questionsGPT for excerpts quoting "same average validity coefficient of .58" or "second-best predictor" and update if Finding 1 fix is approved.
